QUESTION (by fiemmi @ 2026-04-23T16:59:53.718Z):
Dear Combine experts,
with this thread I’d like to report a behavior of the current routine producing impact plots which, I believe, is unwanted and potentially dangerous.
When making expected impact plots, we want to throw an Asimov dataset, fit its nuisance parameters (NPs) back, and plot the differences \hat{\theta}_i - \theta_{i,0}, where \theta_{i,0} is the “initial value” of the i-th NP. According to the very definition of Asimov dataset, we should fit all the NPs back, thus getting \hat{\theta}_i - \theta_{i,0} = 0\, \forall i, which means no pulls whatsoever. Now, what the “initial value” \theta_{i,0} is, really depends on what the analyzer does. If one does
combineTool.py --M Impacts -d datacard.root -t -1 --setParameters <something> --doFits

then the routine takes the values of the NPs in the input workspace, throws an Asimov dataset, fits it back. If the Asimov fit is healthy, the pulls are zero. Now, as soon as one adds --toysFrequentist to the command above, a frequentist fit to the data is triggered in order to make the “best” choice of the NPs values (let’s call them \theta_{\textrm{freq},i}) to throw the Asimov with. But still, when making plots, the values taken by the NPs in the input workspace (\theta_{\textrm{ws},i}) are used as the “initial values”. Clearly, since now a frequentist fit to the data is involved, we will in general have \hat{\theta}_i - \theta_{\textrm{ws},i} \neq 0, and we will thus see pulls, while the correct behavior would be to plot \hat{\theta}_i - \theta_{\textrm{freq},i} (which for a healthy Asimov fit should be 0).
Let me quickly flash why I believe this is potentially dangerous. Suppose you are making a mass measurement (e.g., Higgs mass). You’ll have several energy scale NPs, which effectively are able to shift your mass peak to the left or to the right. Now you fix the value of your parameter of interest MH to a given value (say, 125.38 GeV), make a frequentist fit to the data to get the values of the NPs (this is very common in H(γγ) where we want e.g. to choose the best-fit PDF for background from the data), throw and Asimov dataset, and finally make impact plots. I attach a possible result of such routine down below.
impacts_toysFreq1598×301 44.3 KB
Basically, the scale nuisances are consistently pulled to the right, effectively telling us that the observed mass is higher than 125.38 GeV. Needless to say: despite this being unintended, it is not fine to have this information prior to unblinding…
In conclusion, I think we are definitely not supposed to have access to pulls when the frequentist fit is triggered and, when this is the case, \hat{\theta}_i - \theta_{\textrm{freq},i} should be plotted.
Let me know what you think. Thanks for your time!
All the best,
Fabio.
