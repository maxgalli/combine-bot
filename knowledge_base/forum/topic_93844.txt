Dear Ming-Yan,
I think it is not strictly necessary to look at the post-fit to understand if one-sided impacts make sense. If you see that the pre-fit uncertainty templates are one-sided, i.e. up and down variations are on the same side with respect to the nominal, then having one-sided impacts on the POI that linearly scales the signal yields makes sense. Now, if you see that the shape uncertainty templates are one-sided, then one has to understand if it is a genuine effect or due to bag.

REPLY 3 [ACCEPTED ANSWER] (by amarini @ 2025-01-27T17:50:33.089Z):
Hi Ming-Yan,
it is not super clear what you are trying to achieve or what the reviewer asked you exactly.
In FitDiagnostics you already have the prefit and postfit shapes, where the postfit is after the fit and all nuisances are set to their respective best-fit value.
If you are trying to compare the effect of a single particular systematic on the template/parametric shape (probably prefit, +/-1sigma ?[*]), you can still use FitDiagnostics:
Prefit
combine -M FitDiagnostics [...] -n .nominal --saveShapes --skipBOnlyFit --skipSBFit  #skip fitting
combine -M FitDiagnostics [...] -n .systup --saveShapes --skipBOnlyFit --skipSBFit --setParameters syst=1

fetch the shapes and plot them with root.
-Andrea
[*] but could be also postfit, it is a little bit more involved: you need to save a snapshot and start from there in such a way to have all the other nuisances at the right place.
In this case, you could also use the outcome values of the impact to check particular points. I would suggest in anycase to start from the prefit ones, that will give you more insight.
