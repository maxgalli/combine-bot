Hi Combine experts,
I'm trying to find the prefit covariance matrix to include in the
HEPData package for SUS-20-004 (Search for SUSY with H(bb) + H(bb) +
MET signature).  I'm not getting the results I expect.  I'm following
the instructions here:
https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/viewauth/CMS/SUSPAGPreapprovalChecks
in the section titled "Bin/nuisance variances and covariance matrix ".
The datacard is hhMetABCD_bin1.txt [1].  This card is for one of the 22
analysis bins.  In this bin all yields are >100 events.  We use an ABCD
method, with a correction factor kappa.  Thus the background
prediction is
A_pred = (C/D)*B*kappa.
The A, B, C, and D yields are introduced via rateparams alpha, beta,
gamma, and delta, respectively, with alpha evaluated via the formula:
alpha rateParam A bkg (@0*@1/@2*@3) beta,gamma,delta,kappa.
kappa is introduced as a gaussian param, with uncertainty from MC
statistics.  The systematic uncertainty in kappa is introduced via a
lnN nuisance called cr_ttbar.  It is applied to B, to facilitate profiling.
First, I calculate the prediction by hand:
(C/D)*B*kappa = (1711/2999)*260*1.07 = 158.72
s.d. =
(1711/2999)*260*1.07*sqrt(1/1711+1/2999+1/260+(.0428/1.07)^2+.02^2) = 13.05
Next, I calculate the prediction by profiling the A yield.  The datacard
for this is hhMetABCD_bin1_inv.txt [2], obtained from the master card by
swaping in the two commented-out lines to make alpha a fit variable and beta
a formula.

text2workspace.py hhMetABCD_bin1_inv.txt --channel-masks --X-allow-no-signal

combine hhMetABCD_bin1_inv.root  -M MultiDimFit --algo grid -P alpha

--setParameters mask_A=1 --points 500 --maxDeltaNLLForProf 500 --autoRange 10
-n hhMetBCD_bin1_MDF
In the output, we find
alpha: 158.675 +/- 13.0458.
These two evaluations of the uncertainty in A are in good agreement,
+/- 13.05 events.
Now I try to get the covariance matrix.  In CMSSW_10_2_13, I run the commands.

text2workspace.py hhMetABCD_bin1.txt

combine -M FitDiagnostics -t -1 --expectSignal 0 --rMin -10 hhMetABCD_bin1.root --forceRecreateNLL --saveWithUncertainties --saveOverallShapes --numToysForShape 200 --plots

Hi Bill,
I don't think you're comparing the right things here. The pre-fit
uncertainty on the shapes does not include effects from the rateParams
(the pre-fit uncertainty for those is not defined). So this is why you
are finding a small uncertainty on the pre-fit background rate in bin A
- and something quite different than what you get when you do the fit
with MultiDimFit. If you look at the results in shapes_fit_b these
should be closer to what you got there, but also not exactly the same
because you masked bin A when running MultiDimFit, but not in the
FitDiagnostics call.
So I think the commands you are running are not wrong, but the fully
pre-fit quantities are not well defined in this case.
Best,
Adinda

REPLY 4 (by amarini @ 2021-10-22T16:09:15.000Z):
*** Discussion title: Higgs Combination
Hi William,
I don't think you have to mask anything. Although, probably masking region A is the same as running the s+b fit.
You should simply run with more toys. 200 toys corresponds to a stat. uncertainty on you error estimation from the covariance estimation of ~ +/-1, which is compatible with your expectations but you probably could do better.
As a reminder, the covariance matrix on the observable is obtained by resampling with toys the covariance matrix of the fit, thus the precision you get is related to the number of toys you use. For plotting tools, it is generally good to have only a rough estimate and not be limited by large running time. Since your fit is pretty simple, you can afford to increase the number of toys and benefiting of a better estimation.
Cheers,
-Andrea
