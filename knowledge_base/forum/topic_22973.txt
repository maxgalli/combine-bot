Hi all,
I have a simple binned Higgs combine datacard with a 350 crystal ball peak and a smooth, falling background estimation derived through the ABCD method.
Visual demonstration: https://gitlab.cern.ch/bff-zprime/exo-datacards/-/blob/EXO-22-006/EXO-22-006/test/back_350.png
Example datacard: https://gitlab.cern.ch/bff-zprime/exo-datacards/-/blob/EXO-22-006/EXO-22-006/test/datacard.txt
I want to apply a shape fit uncertainty (~2%) and  lnN closure uncertainty (~20%) to the ABCD background, but when computing AsymptoticLimits, I noticed the effect of the 20% closure uncertainty was reduced from my expectation.
We can see this in the diffNuisances after running  FitDiagnostics:
https://gitlab.cern.ch/bff-zprime/exo-datacards/-/blob/EXO-22-006/EXO-22-006/test/fitResults_t0

name                                              b-only fit            s+b fit         rho  approx impact
back_closure_2016                                +0.00, 0.08     ! +0.00, 0.08!     !-0.13!      -0.001
lumi                                             +0.00, 1.00        -0.00, 1.00       +0.00      +0.000

So, in other words, I am only seeing 8% of my initial 20% uncertainty post fit. This seems to be tied to the size of the statistical uncertainty of the background:
1.Tthe background has around 5000 events, so the statistical uncertainty is sqrt(5000)= 70 events

5000*20%*8%=80 , which is around 70 events

To confirm this, I also ran this with a 500 event background, and found that the post fit uncertainty shifted to match sqrt(500) = 22 events.
Is my understanding of what is going on correct? Either way, is this an issue/what should I do to properly apply the full uncertainty?

REPLY 4 (by anigamov @ 2023-04-24T08:16:02.453Z):
Hi Ryan,
Thanks for updating the links!
I think it is expected that you observe significant constraints with the way things are implemented now and I would say that your understanding of the situation is correct.
But the best way to implement the ABCD background estimation is through the simultaneous fit of A,B,C and D regions. You can parametrise the background rates with rate parameters as described here, for more details take a look at the SUS AN linked from the same page where the ABCD method implementation in combine is described in details.
If someone else has a more recent datacard example with ABCD background  welcome to comment, but I think this example works fine. Hope this helps and let me know if you have more questions.
