QUESTION (by acarvalh @ 2022-04-14T09:07:15.165Z):
Dear experts
I am planning combine several analyses in which results are already public and development ended. Therefore there is no chance of datacards being remade.
Some of those analyses have datacards with signal normalized to one unit (pb) and the results of the individual fits for those will not be stable if just plain normalizing the signal to other unit (fb). The opposite case would happen with other set of the analyses.
Do you think there is a way to circumvent this problem in a combination?
I was thinking a solution could be eg:
Making the combination assuming the signal in pb in adding POI’s to  the analyses that are only stable with signal in fb and fix them to a value that would uniformize signal units. But I am not sure if fit stability problem will still be an issue.
So before trying that I wanted to know if you have better ideas, or if situations like that already happened.
thank you
Xanda

REPLY 4 (by woodson @ 2022-04-14T12:53:04.613Z):
Hi Xanda,
I think the suggestion was to do this:
POI (r) in units of pb
(1) analysis that works well with pb: scale signal with r as normal
(2) analysis that only works well with fb: scale all signal processes with a frozen rateParam with a size 1/1000
Then float the POI r as normal. To analysis (1) it’s as if the POI is in pb; to analysis (2) it’s as if the POI is in fb.
But I now realize that this may not work because it could really come down to what the range of r is. Sometimes the fits are very sensitive to that, especially “close to” the boundaries, because of the nonlinear transformation that MINUIT uses.
So maybe one question is: what are the typical limits (in pb say) that are placed by these two analyses? Are they very different? And what range of r works well (again in pb) for each?
Thanks,
Javier
