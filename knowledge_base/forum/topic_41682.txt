REPLY 2 (by fiemmi @ 2024-05-28T11:46:06.882Z):
Hi Matheus,
generally, speaking, after applying all the selection cuts of your analysis, you should be left with a number of events, predicted by MC. Let’s call this number n_{\textrm{pred}}. Now, this number as is, is a completely arbitrary number, as it depends on how many MC events were generated in the first place. Thus, you should scale n_{\textrm{pred}} in order to obtain the number of expected events corresponding to a give luminosity in data, let’s call it  n_{\textrm{exp}}. The number of expected events is simply obtained from the number of predicted events in MC, scaled by the ratios of luminosities in data and MC:
n_{\textrm{exp}} = n_{\textrm{pred}} \times \frac{L_{\textrm{data}}}{L_{\textrm{MC}}}.
This can be rewritten using the number of generated events in MC, prior to any selections (n_{\textrm{gen}}) and the cross section of the MC sample (\sigma_{\textrm{MC}}), as
n_{\textrm{exp}} = n_{\textrm{pred}} \times \frac{L_{\textrm{data}}}{\frac{n_{\textrm{gen}}}{\sigma_{\textrm{MC}}}} = n_{\textrm{pred}} \times \frac{\sigma_{\textrm{MC}} L_{\textrm{data}}}{n_{\textrm{gen}}},
which is the usual formula we are familiar with. If I understand correctly, this is the formula you are indeed using. There’s one caveat though: if the MC samples are weighted, the n_{\textrm{pred}} and n_{\textrm{gen}} values from MC aren’t simply number of events, but rather sums of weights. That is, n_{\textrm{pred}} is the sum of weights of events passing your selection, and n_{\textrm{gen}} is the sum of weights of all the generated events in the MC sample, prior to any selection. I suspect neither your num_events_generated nor your num_events_processed are sum of events. If your MC sample(s) is(are) weighted, this will lead to incorrect results.
I hope this helps,
best,
Fabio.

I don’t think it is too surprising to see large cross sections from a BSM model with the default parameters, what really matters is the limits you will quote on its production cross section.
Saying, you will get the signal strength “r” which is the POI you are fitting thru combine. Then you multiply that on top of the nominal cross section (the value is 51130 here) and you get the limits on production cross section.
With that being said, combine, being a numerical tool, works good with not too large values. For your background, you can see that you just have percent level uncertainties, O(1) background, then you should expect exclusion limit around 2sigma saying 0.2 (I assume 10% at the end of the day) which gives you 0.2/45000…

REPLY 11 (by sqian @ 2024-05-28T14:40:09.439Z):
actually divide by 100000  (in the past you are fitting “r”, now since you have 1/100000 signal, you are fitting “r * 100000”) So basically, now it is 0.0199068 and in the past you should have 0.0199068 / 100000 since you have 100000x larger signal injected. Note that this would gives you a cross section limit at ~ 50000 fb *  0.0199068 / 100000 which makes more sense to me.
But now you see combine works which is a good sign, perhaps you can try toy limits from now on due to the lack of background stats for a better limit number (Asymptotic formulation breaks down)
For HybridNew method, better check the common command line option out which has the --rMin and --rMax and is relevant for such a Toy based limit calculation.

REPLY 12 (by tvami @ 2024-05-28T14:42:27.965Z):
Matheus Macedo:

combine -d datacard_1000GeV.txt -M HybridNew --LHCmode LHC-limits --name _1000GeV -v 1

With this I think you are unblinding.
I suggest
combine datacard_1000GeV.txt -H AsymptoticLimits -M HybridNew  --expectedFromGrid=0.5 --saveWorkspace --LHCmode LHC-limits -v 1 --rAbsAcc 0.00005 --adaptiveToys 1 -T 5000 --plot=limit_scan.png

-H will use the hint method so you look for r around where the asymptotic would suggest
--expectedFromGrid=0.5 gives you the median expected limit
--rAbsAcc makes sure your accuracy if correct
--adaptiveToys 1 will run less toys at improbable r values
otherwise 5000 toys
the --plot is a very nice diagnostic plot, you should see if the error bars are ok and if there was enough toys, etc

Tamas
