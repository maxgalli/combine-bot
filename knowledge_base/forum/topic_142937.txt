QUESTION (by naislam @ 2026-03-12T12:21:18.764Z):
Dear all,
I am studying regularization after unfolding in our analysis, HIG-25-019. The CADI link is here.
We observe sharp spikes in the 𝛿 scans of the mean global correlation coefficient. From our study it appears that these spikes are linked to increased correlations between the POIs. While individual nuisance parameters tend to reduce the correlations among the POIs, introducing multiple nuisance parameters simultaneously increases these correlations and seems to generate the spikes.
Has anyone encountered a similar effect before? In particular, we would like to understand what could be causing this behavior and what would be the best way to get rid of these spikes.

REPLY 7 (by anigamov @ 2026-04-02T10:40:31.952Z):
Dear Naila,
I think it would be good to check whether these uncertainties have variations that make sense.
Have you tried plotting them? Do they affect the process normalization in the reco bins significantly, or is it mostly shape effect? If they have large normalization effects, it might make sense to normalize the variations, so that they only affect the shape of your observables, especially if you also have background rate parameters in your model.
Consider uploading the datacards to https://gitlab.cern.ch/cms-analysis/hig/hig-25-019/datacards and we take a look.
Hope that helps,
Aliya
