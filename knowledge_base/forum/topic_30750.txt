Dear experts,
in my two Search Regions with 2016 data I have from combine tool the following bump characterization:
SR1                                          SR2mod13

mass, GeV                           28.0+/-0.5                                       28.7+/-1.3
width, GeV                           1.4+/-1.1                                         2.3+/-3.6
significance                          3.0                                                   1.5
When I combined it with
combineCards.py SR1_2016=2016_dimuon_SR1.txt SR2mod13_2016=2016_dimuon_SR2mod13.txt > SR1andSR2mod13.txt
text2workspace.py -P HiggsAnalysis.CombinedLimit.PhysicsModel:multiSignalModel --PO verbose --PO ‘map=SR1_2016/sig:r_sig1[1,0,200]’ --PO ‘map=SR2mod13_2016/sig:r_sig2[1,0,200]’ SR1andSR2mod13.txt -o SR1andSR2mod13.root
combine -M MultiDimFit --setParameters r_sig1=0,r_sig2=0 SR1andSR2mod13.root -v 3 > SR1andSR2mod13.out
I got
mass = 28.042	 +/-  0.407202 GeV
width = 1.3431	 +/-  0.916107  GeV
deltaNLL = 5.52272
quantileExpected = 0.00399497
From deltaNLL I calculate one-sided p-value with two degree of freedom as
pvalue_chi2 = 0.5TMath::Prob(2deltaNLL,2);
and then combined significance as
significance_2ndf = RooStats::PValueToSignificance(pvalue_chi2);
and I got significance_2ndf = 2.87856
So, my naive question, who combined significance 2.90 is smaller than the significance in SR1, 3.0 ?
I would assume it might happen in the case than the masses in SR1 and SR2mod13 are very different,
but it is not my case.  Please, explain.
Thanks, Sasha.

I think that combining 2017 and 2018 data within one SR I still should used
Chernoff formula.  We do not know the signal model and although within a given SR we use the same off-line kinematical selections, there are a number of differences:  trigger (double in 2017 vs single in 2018),  jet pileup ID for forward jets, pileup which affects differently muon off-line isolation. REPLY 8 (by anigamov @ 2023-10-23T09:41:01.459Z):
Dear Sasha,
I think different experimental conditions and the corresponding corrections are usually modelled with separate nuisance parameters, not parameters of interests.
Therefore I would propose to decorrelate the systematic uncertainties in 2017 and 2018 datacards and use the same POI to model signal.

Aliya

REPLY 9 (by anikiten @ 2023-10-23T09:58:46.000Z):
Dear Aliya,
significance and bump characteristics are defined entirely from the data from unbinned fit with the signal and bkg. pdf analitical functions. Therefore we do not have nuisance parameters in this analysis. For the first publications we checked that the formal inclusion in the data cards lumi, muon and b-tagging uncertainty does not change anything in the signal characterization and the significance.
