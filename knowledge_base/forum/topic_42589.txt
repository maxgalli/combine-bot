REPLY 2 (by agilbert @ 2024-06-20T21:37:35.029Z):
Hi Jan Lukas,
Just some quick feedback - not completely sure what’s going on.

For the impacts I would recommend setting the discrete indexes to their known best-fit values and then skipping the discrete profiling (can use --X-rtd MINIMIZER_skipDiscreteIterations).
If that doesn’t help, fits that fail quite randomly by changing initial conditions might point to cases where the Hessian is not very accurate, perhaps due to an odd shape or discontinuities in the likelihood, or strong correlations. One check is to run robustHesse on top of a simple fit and check that the Hessian is inverted successfully - it will also report any strong correlations. Just add --robustHesse 1 to your successful initial fit command. Note that adding this to your impacts command as above won’t help - even if robustHesse runs in this case it won’t be used in the impact calculation.
Have a look at the unprofilled NLL scans and the first and second derivatives: combineTool.py -M FastScan -w workspace.root:w -o fastscan -f multidimfit.root:fit_mdf, see if anything looks odd

cheers,
Andrew
