QUESTION (by pengn1 @ 2022-07-11T12:37:43.000Z):
Dear Experts:
I am trying to use combine tool to perform the background estimation with the ABCD method, which predicts both shape and normalization. The instructions in the following link: https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part2/settinguptheanalysis/#rate-parameters is normalization only, and I find a link to use RooParametricHist to achieve bin by bin estimation.
https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/bin/viewauth/CMS/HiggsWG/SWGuideNonStandardCombineUses#GammaN_with_shapes_using_RooPara
In this case, I’m wondering :
a) if using RooParametricHist is the suggested way to apply the ABCD method bin by bin? or do you have any other suggestions (As the website looks outdated)
b) how should we deal with the shape systematic uncertainties? Implemented by ourselves in the workspace as described in the above link? Could you give us more detailed suggestions?
Thanks advanced for your help!
Best regards,
Na

REPLY 2 (by nckw @ 2022-07-11T12:55:33.000Z):
Dear Na,
I suggest you follow the up-to-date documentation (the Twiki is indeed outdated)
http://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/nonstandard/#rooparametrichist-gamman-for-shapes
You will see there how to include systematic variations via providing up/down templates (RooDataHists) just like you would do for TH1 based analyses.
Best,
Nick
