QUESTION (by gkaratha @ 2024-03-29T12:11:18.387Z):
Dear experts,
I am doing an analysis using a parametric type fit. The background is fitted with few analytical functions following the discrete profiling method. In the simplest case we have two functions: a Chebychev pol and a sum of exponentials. I was making a bias test, were I generate toys with the sum of exponentials and fitted back with the full envelope. For every toy I scan signal strength (r) with 20 points from -5 to 5, saving the NLL of the envelope and the index of the PDF used by the envelope. Then I plot the 2NLL as a function of r for every toy, similar to the plot shown in “advanced use cases”
The result is that in most toys the envelope selects the r with the minimum NLL as one expects, but there is though a subset of toys for which it takes a different point. I am not sure what can cause this.  I am attaching two plots one that we take the minimum NLL and one that is problematic.
The commands I used are here:
To generate toys:
combine -M GenerateOnly param_datacard.root -m 91 -t 10 -n .generate_truth_index_Expo --expectSignal 0 --saveToys -setParameters pdf_index=1 --freezeParameters pdf_index
To scan toys:
combine -M MultiDimFit param_datacard.root -m 91 -t 10 -n .bias_grid_truth_file_fit_envelop --toysFile   --algo grid --points 20  --rMin -5 --rMax 5 --saveNLL --cminDefaultMinimizerStrategy 0 --X-rtd REMOVE_CONSTANT_ZERO_POINT=1 --X-rtd MINIMIZER_freezeDisassociatedParams --X-rtd MINIMIZER_multiMin_hideConstants --X-rtd MINIMIZER_multiMin_maskConstraints --saveWorkspace --saveSpecifiedIndex pdf_index
Any ideas what could cause this behaviour?
Best regards,
George
