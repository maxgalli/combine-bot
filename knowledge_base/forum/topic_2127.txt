Hi Stephanie,
The limits are on the first POI, that is scanned irrespectively if it were fixed or floating.
The test statistics and the CLs are computed for each fixed value of r and the intercept with the 95% is derived.
What do you want to do? Do you want to derive the upper limit on another POI with r freeze / floating?
Or are you trying to derive the expected limits on the Asimov dataset with the signal injected? (-t -1 --setParameter r=1)[*] (you need to look at the observed) (notice that with r=0 the expected median and the observed should be identical).
Cheers,
-Andrea
[*] for multidim model it is better to specify them with setparameters, in order to avoid possible confusions.

Hi,
I don't understand your request or what you are trying to achieve.
Two clarifications:
* My comment on r=0 was referred to the fit on the Asimov dataset.
* The S+B and B fits are two different fits and there is not reason why they should yield the same B prediction. In general, the larger the Signal the larger will be the difference in the B prediction from the two fits.
An extreme example is the to the continuum background under the Z peak.
You should clarify if you are trying to obtain an information from the maximum likelihood fits (S+B or B-only) or from the Asymptotic Limit setting procedure.
I don't understand why comparing the two background predictions in data should be relevant. If you want to study coverage or bias properties, it is better done on Asimov or pseudo experiments of known datasets.

Cheers,
-Andrea

Hi Stephanie,
p-values are computed with combine in many ways depending on the method used and the specific setting selected.
They can range from deltaLogLikelihood interpreted as p-value under the regularity assumption of Wilks theorem (Likelihood scan), using the asymptotic formulae for the modified test statistics used in upper limits, to the derivation of test-statistics with pseudo experiments (e.g GoF, Hybrid limits, FC confidence intervals, ...) . The latter really depends on the methods used and how toys are generated.
You can refer to the manual page for detailed information of each method and options available.
http://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/commonstatsmethods.html
Cheers,
-Andrea
