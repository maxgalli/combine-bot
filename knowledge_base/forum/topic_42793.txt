REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2024-06-26T13:38:10.872Z):
Dear Jihun,
If in the 2nd case the only signal process you have scales with r^2 then the plot that you obtain for the impacts is consistent, right? That is, whether r is above 0 or below 0, in both cases it increases the signal yield wrt mu=0 (from 0 to something positive). It can never compensate for the effect of varying a systematic uncertainty that is moved in such a way that it increases the background rate (such that the nominal yield in the asimov dataset is below the value you would get for that bkg uncertainty). In such a case you would then end up with a one-sided impact.
Note that I’m not commenting here on whether it makes sense to have a signal scaled by r^2 - this depends on the details of your analysis, which I did not look into. I’m purely commenting on what you get given this model construction.
Regarding bins with negative contents: I would suggest rebinning to ensure the bin contents are not negative. If you have minor backgrounds which have small negative bin contents, you could also consider setting those to 0. Or you could try to generate a larger MC sample, if this is possible. In short, there are several ways to address the issue, which one is the best depends on your situation.
Hope that helps,
Adinda

REPLY 4 (by adewit @ 2024-06-26T14:59:19.507Z):
Dear Jihun,
On 1: This is also my interpretation, yes - the minimizer makes a first step in r and keeps going in the same direction, so depending on what that first step is it picks positive or negative r
On the 2nd point: If you decide to ignore some minor contributions I would also remove the systematic variations. Note that you should only remove minor contributions if they are minor with respect to the other backgrounds in that bin - not just ‘small with respect to 0’. If there are very few other backgrounds then it would be better to simply rebin.
Best,
Adinda
