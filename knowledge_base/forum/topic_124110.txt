REPLY 15 (by gortona @ 2025-05-05T15:32:46.000Z):
Hi Sasha,
For lacking of a better terminology, I’m calling bestfit the result if the minimisation procedure from the first command, and bestfit_with_your_parameters the evaluation of the likelihood function of the best fit once you add the setParameters option.
What you say is correct, in the second command your inputs are used to recalculate the S+B NLL, but in the first command they are not.
So you should be getting from the first command: NLL(bestfit - prefit) and from the second command NLL(bestfit_with_your_parameters - prefit) (from the NLL branches in the output trees) as well as the deltaNLL between the B-only fit with your parameters and bestfit_with_your_parameters. With these 3 inputs you should have all the information to extract the difference between B-only and bestfit, which is what you are looking for.
Cheers,
Giacomo

Hi Sasha,
For lacking of a better terminology, I’m calling bestfit the result if the minimisation procedure from the first command, and bestfit_with_your_parameters the evaluation of the likelihood function of the best fit once you add the setParameters option.
What you say is correct, in the second command your inputs are used to recalculate the S+B NLL, but in the first command they are not.
So you should be getting from the first command: NLL(bestfit - prefit) and from the second command NLL(bestfit_with_your_parameters - prefit) (from the NLL branches in the output trees) as well as the deltaNLL between the B-only fit with your parameters and bestfit_with_your_parameters. With these 3 inputs you should have all the information to extract the difference between B-only and bestfit, which is what you are looking for.
Cheers,
Giacomo
