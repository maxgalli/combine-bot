REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2024-09-09T07:44:46.488Z):
Hi Shriniketan,
When you run the limits with -t -1 (note that --run blind would be better), the asimov data set that is used for calculating the expected limits is built from the pre-fit expectation, ie with all nuisance parameters at their nominal values. Without -t -1, a fit to data is performed (with the signal strength at 0) and the asimov dataset is constructed assuming the values of the fitted parameters. It is thus not unexpected that the expected limits can change between the two configurations.
If you haven’t done so yet, you might also want to have a look at previous Combine tutorials (e.g. the exercises here: https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/latest/part5/longexercise/ or the longer tutorials linked from here: https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/latest/part4/usefullinks/), where your can learn a bit more about what happens in the limit setting procedure and what the functions of the different options are.
