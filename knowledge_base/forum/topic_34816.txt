QUESTION (by jinw @ 2024-02-15T11:26:18.316Z):
Dear experts,
I’m investigating the impacts of the systematic uncertainties on my fit to the signal rate parameter in the range [0,20], and saw at the 1sigma and -1sigma impacts of most of the systematic sources are on the same side (impact plot here http://submit08.mit.edu/~jinw65/impacts/impacts_scaledownsig100.pdf). This indicates that the change of the nuisances on both sides will shift the signal rate to the same side. I wonder if the impacts make sense and if there are potential issues in the fit.
Further debugging:
In the likelihood scan of the signal rate r, I have seen the minimum at a negative value. Since a negative signal is not physical in the analysis, we constrained it to be larger than 0 in the fits of asymptotic limit, FitDiagnostics and MultiDimFit. All these fits converged if r>0. The NLL plots from FastScan are here http://submit08.mit.edu/~jinw65/impacts/nll.pdf.
Thanks for your help!
Weijie

Did you check whether the one sided issue could be resolved by allowing r to be negative? Like ‘ — rMin -20’

REPLY 3 (by gortona @ 2024-02-15T12:06:39.000Z):
Dear Weijie,
I think the impacts make sense given that you fit a value very close to the boundary.
In these conditions one thing you can do to check your impacts is to inject an artificial signal (r=1 for example) and see how the expected (—expectSignal=1 -t -1) impacts behave. If they are still one-sided than something is fishy,
Cheers,
Giacomo

REPLY 6 (by gortona @ 2024-02-18T21:28:37.000Z):
Dear Weijie,
When you say you had to increase the cminDefaultMinimizerTolerance, you mean that you set values larger than 0.1? In any case there’s nothing magical on the default value of this setting. It’s a value that we find works in most cases, but it can well be that in your case you need to change some of the minimiser options, in principle that’s not a red flag, although it does point to some potential instability in the fit. You might want to check the likelihood shape you get from -M MultiDimFit —algo=grid to see if the likelihood shape is regular.
I would say most of the nuisance are well behaved. I would check if it makes sense that cms_trk_kill is one-sided, and I see there are a few bin-by-bin uncertainties one-sided as well. This would make sense if the corresponding bins have total yields close to 0 (since they could only fluctuate upwards). But overall imho I don’t see anything too worrisome.
Best regards,
Giacomo
