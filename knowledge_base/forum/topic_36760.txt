REPLY 2 (by amarini @ 2024-03-13T08:09:43.627Z):
Not exactly,
you would need to remove the subtractions that combine does under the hood (as explained by Nick) with --X-rtd REMOVE_CONSTANT_ZERO_POINT=1.
The only relevance I see for us is to show the relative shift of the pdf in the discrete profile method (see here).
Furthermore, be aware that, depending on the datacard, there may be additional constant values subtracted by root/roofit, as well. For instance, it is unlikely that the factorial terms (n!) is ever evaluated in the Poisson distributions when (e.g.) a likelihood is intended to be used.
Furthermore, notice that in likelihood based statistical procedures, likelihood ratios carries the information used for statistical inference.
Cheers,
-Andrea
