QUESTION (by afiqaize @ 2023-08-15T14:17:57.362Z):
Hi experts,
I am referring to the following remark in [1]:
[...] and then optionally specify what are the names of the signal and background pdfs, e.g. --signalPdfNames='ggH*,vbfH*' and --backgroundPdfNames='*DY*,*WW*,*Top*' (by default, the definitions of signal and background are taken from the datacard).

In my case, I have signal processes A, B, C, where C is a signal for strictly technical reasons, in that it is scaled by a parameter that needs to be treated as a POI. From physics point of view, C is considered part of the background. For this reason, it is desirable to be able to reassign C to the total_background histogram produced by FitDiagnostics. Based on [1] I expected that using the options above as --signalPdfNames='A,B' --backgroundPdfNames='C,other_bkg_procs' would achieve exactly what I want, but the resulting total_signal/background histograms generated with and without the option are identical, and conceptually this is incorrect.
Is there a way to do what I want, correctly accounting for all relevant correlations?
Cheers,
Afiq
[1] https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/nonstandard/#plotting

REPLY 2 (by kcormier @ 2023-08-15T17:42:00.034Z):
Hi Afiq,
I believe that --signalPdfNames and --backgroundPdfNames are only affecting the plots themselves, i.e. the png outputs; not actually affecting the individual histograms stored in the root file. So it’s not surprising that the histograms are unchanged.
I don’t think that there is a way only change which processes contribute to the total_signal/total_background histograms. But I think there are a few ways to do what you want, depending on exactly what the goal is.
Maybe I haven’t understood the technical difficulty with calling C a background process, but is there a reason that you can’t keep it as a background process but apply a floating nuisance parameter for its normalization?
Best,
Kyle
