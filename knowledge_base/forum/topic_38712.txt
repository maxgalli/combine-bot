REPLY 6 (by kcormier @ 2024-04-12T07:25:13.995Z):
Hi Cesare,
I’m not sure if I understood what you meant in your first paragraph correctly, but I think that what you’re saying is that you would like your pseudodata generation to also include fluctuations due to the MC statistical uncertainty (and not just from the poisson fluctuations of the model). This can be done by nuisance parameter fluctuations when generating the observed pseudodata, you just need to make sure those nuisance parameters are in the model.
For the second part, the way the models are constructed, there is no explicit subtraction. Instead, the model fits the likelihood of its parameters given the data. This includes all the model parameters, in particular those that affect the background normalization of the MC estimated backgrounds as well as those for the ABCD estimate. This has the advantage of simultaneously taking into account the entire model, rather than e.g. subtracting some fixed number of events from the data first.  So if your goal was to estimate e.g. some parameter of the ABCD method, or the ABCD yields on the data, you would setup your full model, including your other backgrounds from MC, and then set whichever parameters you are interested in as your POIs.
Best,
Kyle
