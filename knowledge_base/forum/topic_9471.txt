REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2022-04-20T10:30:21.572Z):
Dear Julie,
There are several options in PostFitShapesFromWorkspace so without the exact command it’s difficult to confirm if this is correct, however given the uncertainty is smaller than from the individual components I assume that the covariance matrix resampling was switched on (this is the correct behaviour - it is on by default)
It is possible for the total background uncertainty to be smaller than the uncertainties on individual components due to correlations between the parameters in the fit, so in that sense I don’t find the result alarming. However, if you’d like to double check and be sure that the uncertainties on the individual components and on the total are extracted in exactly the same way, the easiest thing to do is check out the HEAD of the Combine 102x branch, where the total signal and background normalizations are now added to the FitDiagnostics output when run with --saveNormalizations --saveWithUncertainties.
Hope this helps,
Adinda

REPLY 3 (by ncsmith @ 2022-04-20T20:18:45.265Z):
I have some old code for this, that basically does the same resampling of the fit covariance that is done to make the shapes in FitDiagnostics, and then propagates that to the per-channel process normalization parameters (i.e. integral of the shapes) and dumps it to a TTree, one row per sample. Then you can loop over the tree and compute mean and std of any combination of normalizations https://gitlab.cern.ch/ncsmith/monoZ/blob/master/plotter/resampleFit.py
