QUESTION (by isalazar @ 2025-11-04T19:52:05.846Z):
Hi all,
We have been encountering some issues when running Combine for our Run 2 analysis “Search for a light pseudoscalar Higgs in H→aa→μμττ final state” (SUS-23-005) and would appreciate your advice. Here is the CADI link.
Our analysis includes four final states: TauHadTauHad (the most dominant channel), TauMuTauHad, TauMuTauE, and TauETauHad, and considers five different Higgs mass (m_H) points. The fits are performed in three “a” mass regions:

Low-mass: 3.6–8 GeV

Upsilon: 8–12 GeV

High-mass: 12–30 GeV

The main issue appears for the m_H = 500 GeV case when we calculate and plot the limits for the all-channels, all-years combined scenario: the observed limits lie mostly within the –2σ band, which seems unexpected.
When checking the individual years and channels, none of them show this behavior. However, two cases stand out:

When plotting all channels, all years combined except TauHadTauHad, the deficit disappears.

For TauHadTauHad, all years combined, the observed limits go well below the expected bands.

Following the suggestion from the NPS conveners, we focused on the problematic points in the upsilon region. We have produced impacts and Hybrid new limit values for ma = 10 GeV. The datacards and workspaces have been carefully verified, but the issue persists. All related files can be found in this CERN Box folder. The script used to combine the datacards is also included in the datacards/ directory for reference (run_combine.csh).
Could you please advise on what might be causing this or suggest further debugging steps?  We can share additional logs or plots if needed.
Thanks a lot for your time and help,

REPLY 13 (by sbaradia @ 2026-03-05T20:30:36.859Z):
Dear all,
Following up on the H500 issue discussed earlier in this thread: we have now identified the origin of the deficit observed in the all-channels, all-years combination. The problem was traced to a subtle bug during the workspace combination. Different channels use different four-body mass  and/or two-body mass ranges in the 2-D fit, but the observables had the same name across channels. During the combination step, the observable range was inherited from the last combined channel, which effectively excluded events outside that range from the likelihood.
We have now fixed this by assigning channel-specific observable names, so that each channel preserves its intended observable range during the combination. After regenerating the limits with this fix, the combined results behave as expected and the apparent deficit in the H500 case disappears.
Below we show the H500 combined limit before and after the fix for reference.
Thanks,
Sweta
