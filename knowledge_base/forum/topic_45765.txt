REPLY 4 (by adewit @ 2024-08-20T16:11:38.589Z):
Dear Nicolò,
Thanks for sharing the files. I confirm your observation that what is printed to the screen looks like the fit working fine, but only 2 out of 3 entries appearing in the output file. However, cranking up the verbosity (even to -v 1) I see that the last fit that’s performed actually fails. I expect this is why the entry doesn’t appear in the output tree.
I quickly scanned this nuisance parameter (using --algo grid) and the shape of the likelihood as a function of this parameter seems a little odd (there are possibly multiple minima). You could investigate this more with the FastScan method of CombineTool (https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/latest/part3/debugging/?h=fastscan#analyzing-the-nll-shape-in-each-parameter). As for the reasons for this: is the uncertainty highly asymmetric for some processes? It’s not the only cause of such issues, but could explain what you are seeing.
Best,

REPLY 6 (by amarini @ 2024-08-22T16:25:18.274Z):
Hi Nicolo’,
Yes, multiple minima can be a problem in fit convergence, as minimum finding algorithms usually try to find local minima.
With Asymmetric uncertainties, we usually mean both the effect that can be induced by a same-sized variation (as the one you pointed out), but also large asymmetric effects between the Up and Down variation, like (possibly) the second bin of slide 7. (Further details are here )
A tool developed a while ago by Adinda for this purpose was ValidateCards. It may point out against the more problematic regions that are worth an investigation.
All these effects induce a non-linear behavior in the expected number of events (vs the nuisance parameters), thus possible multiple minima in the likelihood function.
Changing the step size or the initial value can change the convergence of the fit (for the reason explained above), but it may not be easy to find a stable configuration.
-Andrea
