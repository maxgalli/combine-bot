Dear experts,
I’m working on TOP-24-013, the measurement of a certain forward-backward asymmetry in the single top quark t-channel process, and I’m stuck on impact calculations due to fit failure.
More precisely, the following two commands with this file (higgsCombineMLUnfold.MultiDimFit.mH125.root) fail with a fit failure.
combine -M MultiDimFit -n _paramFit_Test_lumi_13TeV_1718 --algo impact --redefineSignalPOIs r -P lumi_13TeV_1718 --floatOtherPOIs 1 --saveInactivePOI 1 -t -1 --robustFit 1 --cminDefaultMinimizerStrategy 0 -m 125 -d higgsCombineMLUnfold.MultiDimFit.mH125.root --setParameters r=0,sigShapeScale=1 --verbose 3

combine -M MultiDimFit -n _paramFit_Test_pdf_13 --algo impact --redefineSignalPOIs r -P pdf_13 --floatOtherPOIs 1 --saveInactivePOI 1 -t -1 --robustFit 1 --cminDefaultMinimizerStrategy 0 -m 125 -d higgsCombineMLUnfold.MultiDimFit.mH125.root --setParameters r=0,sigShapeScale=1 --verbose 3

It seems the pull estimation works, but the impact calculation fails; see https://cernbox.cern.ch/s/I1syVTgH39Elfd2, having the log from the first command (for lumi_13TeV_1718). I tried several options (thanks for the help), but nothing has cured the failure. One thing I really don’t understand is that the uncertainties lumi_13TeV_1718 and pdf_13 are very tiny, but their impacts are not calculated.
(The datacard for this study can be found in [1], and it directly shows the recipe to construct higgsCombineMLUnfold.MultiDimFit.mH125.root and the commands shared in the above.)
So, I’m trying to investigate it further to draw the NLL of the impact estimation. But I have no idea how to watch the NLL and the statistical model for the impact estimation from the impact algorithm. Is there a way for this?
