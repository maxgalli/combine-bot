QUESTION (by jfriesen @ 2024-10-02T13:58:44.504Z):
Hi all,
I have been running into a lot of trouble getting MultiDimFit to converge. My current setup involves a combined card for 40 bins/histograms, fitting each with 6 background shapes which individually have 1-2 free params, at most. I have been using FastScan and see reasonably shaped NLLs, and fitting a bin individually will usually converge. However, the combined fit fails, and a look at the verbose output usually shows that the minimizer EDM was decreasing before jumping to a very large number and getting stuck.
Beyond checking for minima in the NLL and making sure the shapes are behaving well, are there other reasons for this kind of behavior? I have tried different minimizer strategies, and sometimes one works while another doesn’t, but nothing consistently appears to perform better. Sometimes a very high tolerance works, but I am not sure what the best practice is for this.

REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2024-10-07T14:38:04.843Z):
Hi Jaren,
I’m not sure I fully understand your fit setup (sharing a minimal failing example, if you have one, could be helpful!). It’s generally not recommended to change the minimizer settings ‘until things work’, so it is indeed good to understand why this is happening. You say that the fit will usually converge for individual bins; do you have many parameters that are correlated between the different bins?
You mention free parameters, are these systematic uncertainties that you have applied to your background processes, or parameters of a parametric background model, or something else?
If I understood it correctly, you are fitting 40 categories together, and fits of the individual categories converge most of the time. It could make sense to test sub-combinations of the categories to try to find a more minimal configuration in which the fit does not converge, which could make debugging easier. Alternatively if you have individual categories in which the fit does not converge, you could try to see if a combined fit excluding those categories converges - if so you can simply investigate the individual categories. In that case it would be interesting to check whether any of your parameters are strongly anticorrelated in the fit, which could lead to problems.
Hope this helps,
Adinda
