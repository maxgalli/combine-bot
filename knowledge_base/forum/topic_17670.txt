REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2022-11-21T17:01:45.407Z):
Dear Nicolò,
To answer the question on slide 9: yes, there is a substantial difference between what you show on slide 8 and slide 9: in slide 8, r_S and r_higgs are floating while on slide 9 they are fixed. On slide 8 you didn’t specify explicitly that r_S and r_higgs should float freely, but they do as they don’t have a constraint in the workspace. You would obtain the same result by using --redefineSignalPOIs r_higgs,r_S,r_A -P r_A --floatOtherPOIs 1 (and this would be the recommended way to ensure the other POIs are really floating)
As to why the dNLL plateaus - to me it looks like for extreme values of the asymmetry, the WH cross section is taken almost down to 0 (you can save the value of r_S in the output tree with --trackParameters r_S). For r_S=0 you get deltaNLL ~0.25, and at this point you stop depending on r_A   - so I think what you are seeing is consistent.
Best,
Adinda
