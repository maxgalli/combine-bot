REPLY 2 (by gmelachr @ 2026-04-06T23:01:03.869Z):
Hello,
I kept investigating the situation and I think I understood what is happening. I noticed that all the bins that suffer from this behavior have the same functional form for the background component, which is a single-sided crystal ball. I opened the workspace and I noticed this:
image1071×354 214 KB
All the other pdfs are defined in their classes as f(x,p1,p2…) while the single-sided CB is f(m,p1,p2…), So, in the same bin some pdfs are f(x) and one pdf is f(m). Therefore, I replaced the single-sided CB with a double-sided CB (which depends on x) and the pre-fit and the post-fit pdfs and normalisations are in agreement.
image1052×1071 113 KB
Therefore, in order to avoid similar confusions in the future, I would propose to update the class of the RooCBShape and replace the dependense on m with a dependence on  x.
Best,
Giorgos

Hi Giorgos,
Maybe I’m missing something, but in principle the label “m” in RooCBShape is just a dummy internal label for the observable, it shouldn’t make any difference whether it is “x” or “m”. What matters is that it maps on to the same RooRealVar, which seems like it in your case.
If you have a minimal failing example (including plotting script), I could try to take a look at it.
