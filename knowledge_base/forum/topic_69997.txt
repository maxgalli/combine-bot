QUESTION (by johornun @ 2024-11-28T13:54:45.296Z):
Hello,
I am performing an analysis looking for a decay of the SM Higgs boson into two pseudoscalar Higgs bosons subsequently decaying into kaons in the ZH production mode of  the SM Higgs boson. Since I am new to particle physics, I initially based my analysis on the Parametric Exercise on the Combine website and went from there. My question now regards a difference between my resulting workflow and the workflow detailed in the exercise.
In my workflow, I normalize my signal MC distribution to cs*br/N_events. Then I mutiply that normalization weight with scale factors and luminosity together to obtain an overall event weight. After that, I fit my model to the weighted MC distribution. Finally, I save that model together with the sum of weights as {model}_norm into a workspace and set the signal rate in the datacard to 1. With the asymptotic method of combine, I get an observed limit O(0.01pb) using that workflow. The parametric exercise performs the same workflow (minus weights) for the background estimation.
But for the signal estimation the parametric exercise does something different. It also fits a model to a weighted signal MC distribution; however, the weights seem to exclude the luminosity. After that, the exercise calculates an efficiency eff = sumw/(cs*br) and then a norm N = cs*br*eff*lumi. This norm is put into the datacard as signal rate. If I calculate a norm, like the parametric exercise does, it is equal to the norm calculated with my approach, but the resulting observed limit with the asymptotic method comes out to be O(0.1pb).
So, I do have two approaches, that give the same intermediate result but two different final results. I hope you are able to solve this discrepancy.
Cheers
Johannes Hornung

REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2024-11-28T14:13:42.677Z):
Hi Johannes,
I think what you should be putting in the datacard for the signal, if you set the {model}_norm to weights*xs*br/Nevents, is the value of the luminosity. The main point is that {model}_norm and the rate in the datacard are multiplied. So you could set {model}_norm to 1 and the rate in the datacard to N=weights*xs*br*lumi/Nevents, {model}_norm to N and the rate in the datacard to 1, or something in between, as long as rate*{model}_norm = N.
Perhaps you are double counting some of the factors in your setup, ie including them both in rate and in the {model}_norm ?
Hope this helps,
Adinda

REPLY 7 (by adewit @ 2024-11-28T17:01:34.416Z):
Dear Johannes,
Indeed from a quick look the signal normalization looks the same in both cases. However, the signal shapes look somewhat different - this is not the full source of the difference but would have some effect.
The other thing I noticed is that it looks like floating or freezing your systematic uncertainties changes the limits a lot in the ...limitsSanityCheck... case, but do nothing in the ...limits... case - and comparing the limit from the ...limits... case with that obtained with the uncertainties frozen in the ...limitsSanityCheck... case yields two very similar numbers (not identical, because the input signal pdfs are not exactly the same)
In summary, there are probably several things different between your two setups

signal pdfs not exactly the same
likely something different in the systematics implementation

Could you double check the implementation of your systematic uncertainties? I don’t obviously see from the workspace what the differences are, but since these mostly seem to be lognormal uncertainties, you should be able to spot any differences between the two datacards.
Then, for convincing yourself that you get the same result with the two normalization implementations, you can try using the exact same datacard and input workspace in both cases, except flipping the contents of the _norm term with the rate in the datacard, and then things should match. You can even do it without systematics, if it’s not clear to you what the difference in their implementation between the two fits is.
Best,
Adinda
