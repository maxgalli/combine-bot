REPLY 2 [ACCEPTED ANSWER] (by kcormier @ 2024-08-05T16:20:28.918Z):
Dear Hyeon-Seo,
Nuisance parameters such as those introduced as lnN or shape uncertainties do indeed affect the yields of the processes as you had previously understood. I think the intent of the sentence you quote is not ‘fixed’ to the nominal yields, but rather ‘constrained to vary around their nominal yields, only as allowed by their respective nuisance parameters’.
I hope that clarifies things.
Best,

REPLY 4 (by amarini @ 2024-08-05T19:01:34.638Z):
Hi Hyeon-Seo,
There is probably a little bit of confusion on what a fit or a nuisance is supposed to do.
shape uncertainties modify the process normalization with a log-normal uncertainty (as well as the shape). Thus, I we can here discuss only log-normal uncertainty effect.
The shape/rate specification with -1 or with the actual value as integral is identical, and it refers to the prefit normalization. The -1 will read your 1389 from the histogram, instead specifying it manually.  Nuisance parameters may allow to modify a normalization (postfit) with (eventually) a penalty term related to how much the nuisance is pulled.
In your example, the likelihood is going to be a Poisson likelihood with a rate value of 1389, given the observation (n_obs).
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{P}(\lambda = 1389 + \dots |n_\text{obs}) (the + will refer to the parts with the actual pameters, like the signal strength r)
If you include a log-normal uncertainty on that process (let’s say eg with a kappa of k=1.05), you allow this 1389 to be slightly different and can vary within ~5%.
This means that an additional parameter is added to the likelihood and (approx) constraint within 5% to allow this to happen.  The likelihood is going to be:
\mathcal{L}=\mathcal{P}( \lambda \cdot k^\theta + \dots| n_\text{obs}) \mathcal{N}(\theta,0,1).     (Now, you have one more parameter, \theta)
As you can see, the prefit value (\lambda) didn’t change, but postfit the nuisance parameter \theta can move and thus also the best fit value for you yields. The nuisance parameter is not free to float (as for the rate parameters), but it is actually constraint to a gaussian distribution.
Cheers,
-Andrea
