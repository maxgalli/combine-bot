REPLY 3 (by adewit @ 2024-02-16T16:20:12.370Z):
Dear Younghoon,
It is true that Combine will complain in the case of negative event yields (pdfs cannot be negative, after all). This is also consistent in the sense that in general, a negative event yield does not make sense for a physical process (except in the case of negative interference, in which case a specific physics model needs to be used to address the issue).
In your case, you should work out whether a negative yield for this specific process means you have no background from it at all (in which case you might exclude it), or whether your estimate is unreliable (in which case you may need to find an alternative method for estimating it).
Best,
Adinda
