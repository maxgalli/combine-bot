QUESTION (by sajmal @ 2023-08-10T10:35:46.699Z):
Hi everyone,
I’m currently working on a shape-based analysis using templates that were produced with Run 2 statistics. I’m interested in understanding if it’s possible to rescale the analysis results to account for a different integrated luminosity scenario, such as HL-LHC.
Specifically, I’m wondering if the combine tool offers a way to rescale the results to different luminosities while considering the impact on the significance, background rates, and systematic uncertainties.
If anyone has experience with this or can provide guidance on how to correctly handle luminosity rescaling in combine, I’d greatly appreciate your insights. Are there specific options or commands I should use?
Thank you in advance for your expertise and advice!
regards
Sehar

REPLY 2 (by gortona @ 2023-08-10T11:47:36.707Z):
Dear Sehar,
You can apply a rateParam to all processes to scale them to the desired luminosity, such as
lumiScale rateParam * * NNN
to increase the yield of all processes by a factor NNN. I would also suggest to freeze the lumiScale parameter when running combine.
It is also possible to scale constraints on nuisance parameters as well, to match some expected effects, see for example:
http://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/nonstandard/#scaling-constraints
I hope it helps,
Best,
Giacomo
