QUESTION (by fernanpe @ 2025-04-07T11:23:09.814Z):
Dear Combine experts,
During the ARC review of HIG-24-011 we have been asked to get the probability of excluding background-only with toys. In our case, background-only means no off-shell H->WW production.
I followed the instructions here: https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/latest/part3/commonstatsmethods/?h=significance#computing-significances-with-toys
with the following commands:

Generate toys with no signal: combine -M HybridNew datacard_combined_012j.txt --LHCmode LHC-significance --saveToys --fullBToys --saveHybridResult -t 2 --expectSignal=0 -s $1

Get the signal significance: combine -M HybridNew datacard_combined_012j.txt --LHCmode LHC-significance --readHybridResult --toysFile input.root

Plot the test statistic distribution: python3 $CMSSW_BASE/src/HiggsAnalysis/CombinedLimit/test/plotTestStatCLs.py -i higgsCombineTest_merged.root -o test_statistic_distribution.png -m 120 -p r --signif --save-as-pdf

Results:
Significance: -2.15031 -0.00454303/+0.00449908
Null p-value: 0.984235 ± 0.000178683.
Test statistic distribution: https://twiki.cern.ch/twiki/pub/CMS/HWWOffshellFullRun2/test_statistic_distribution.png_q0_120.0.pdf
The significance of -2 sigmas looks expected, as we have a data deficit of about 2 sigmas w.r.t. the expectation in the two most sensitive bins. However, the p-value and the test statistic distribution look off.
For making toys, I submitted to condor 500 jobs with 2 toys each, where each job has a different seed -s. I have spent some time inspecting the limit tree inside the output files, but I am afraid I do not really understand what is stored there (there are very small numbers sometimes, and some values are duplicated).
Could you please a look at the procedure and our files [1] and let us know if there is something wrong or maybe any misinterpretation? I can give you access to the folder where I have the shapes/ root files if needed.
We would appreciate your help. We will not be able to move to the next stage of the review until this is understood.
Thanks,
Pedro, for the authors
