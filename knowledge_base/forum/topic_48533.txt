REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2024-09-13T08:43:57.718Z):
Dear Jihun,
I cannot reproduce your problem in the most recent combine version (notice that it’s recommended to check out a specific version, not the main branch - although in this case neither lead to a crash in FastScan).
However, when I look at your log file I see references to SLC7. The latest version of Combine is meant to be built under EL9, not SLC7. Make sure to also use CMSSW_14_1_0_pre4 as recommended in the documentation (assuming you are running within CMSSW)
For the impacts, in principle I wouldn’t expect there to be a difference between the two approaches that you describe. However if you make one single histogram with all three signal regions concatenated, in case there are normalization uncertainties that have different sizes in the different regions, this is not as easy to take into account (they have to be passed as shape uncertainties too in that case). Did you make those modifications, and did you check that the shape uncertainties make sense? For the 2nd plot, with the impact that you say is one-sided, this looks like there was a fit failure (ie the uncertainty on the positive side was not found, and therefore the impact was not calculated). You could try to scan just that parameter, modifying the fit range slightly, to see if that helps it converge. I think the bigger problem however is the fact that CF is constrained in the first case, but not in the second. You may want to compare the implementations of this parameter between your two fit configurations, to see if you can spot a difference.
Best,
Adinda
