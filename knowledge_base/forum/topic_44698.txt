REPLY 2 (by gortona @ 2024-07-30T12:31:26.806Z):
Dear Koushik,
About the statistical uncertainties, can you please clarify if you mean the proper statistical uncertainty on the process yield, or the effect of limited MC sample size, which actually is a systematic uncertainty?
Uncertainty on processes yields are computed directly by combine according to a Poissonian distribution, so you should not include them explicitly in the datacard, otherwise you will double count them.
About your questions:
i. For processes with a zero event yield, you should use Gamma distributions for your systematic uncertainties instead of log-normal. For the statistical one, it will be handled by combine.
ii. In general, I would advise against having processes with negative yields, especially if such a prediction is clearly unphysical. I would rather set it to 0 with a larger systematic uncertainty or you need to write a proper physics model to deal with it http://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part2/physicsmodels/#interference, but in this case it looks to me a bit of an overkill. You should in any case make sure that the total PDF never goes negative.
iii. It is abit surprising to me that including a few uncertainties in a counting datacard has a visible effect on combine processing time. In any case, if you want to use the autoMCStats feature you can build a 1-bin TH1 histogram for each process (with the correct uncertainty attached) and then build a shape datacard from it.
About the CR usage, the sintax looks correct to me. The way you wrote you will force WJets and TTbar to scale together in the CR, and this scaling will be the same applied to all the SRs. Is this the intended behaviour?
ii. From the point of view of combine it is perfectly fine to merge the 2 processes. Note that in this case ttbar will become almost negligible. I don’t know If this makes sense from the point of view of the analysis or in the context of the PAG.
iii. Combine will always fit the CR and SR together, so it will not make any distinctions. This is the whole point of having CR inside combine. Since the signal contribution in the CR should be negligible, the presence of the CR should only affect the background determination (there are some commands to exclude some channels/regions from the fit, but this is another topic).
I hope it helps,
Best regards,
Giacomo
