QUESTION (by friti @ 2023-05-12T13:35:59.336Z):
Dear Experts,
I am computing the impacts for an Asimov fit and I want to freeze some of the nuisances, but I don’t quite understand the output.
I would have expected the frozen parameters to not be present in the impacts plot, or to be at the very bottom of the list (with zero impact on the result). But this is not the case, in fact they are still present and with high impact on the result, just in different positions.
As expected though, the fit sensitivity is different in the two cases.
Do you know if there is there something I am doing wrong, or that I am not understanding?
Thanks a lot in advance.
Best regards,
Federica Riti

The commands that I use to freeze the parameters when I compute the impacts are here [2], and here [3] the datacard. The nuisances that I freeze are all the ones with error bars (all shape and lnN ones): only the rateParam and flatParam are kept floating.

REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2023-05-12T14:55:10.300Z):
Hi Federica,
I don’t see a mistake in your commands -  when you run with --robustFit the freezing of a parameter is, for the purposes of its own impact scan, effectively disabled. In the fits for the other parameters, so the resulting impacts for the parameters that you didn’t set to frozen are still correct.
What you can do in this case is run the same commands, but add the list of parameters that you want to run the impacts for to the --named flag, or alternatively you could add the list of frozen parameters to the --exclude flag when you run the impact fits. See here: https://github.com/cms-analysis/CombineHarvester/blob/main/CombineTools/python/combine/Impacts.py#L33-L37
Hope this helps,
Adinda

REPLY 4 (by mpieri @ 2023-05-15T07:25:57.000Z):
Dear Analyzers,
to follow up from our last meeting, we think that beyond the answers already given to Alexei and Ulascan, we would like to see refined the treatment of the systematic uncertainties related to the fakes.
From our point of view the most important (partly) missing uncertainties are:

A bin by bin uncertainty from the MC closure, corresponding to the sum in quadrature of the statistical uncertainty in the estimate, the statistical uncertainty of the fakes and the difference between estimate and fakes in Fig. 28 of AN V17. These uncertainties should also affect the category migration.

This first step should be relatively fast to implement and it woudl be good to understand the results to have an idea of how much the limited MC statistics would affect the sensitivity of the analysis.

The other missing point for us the uncertainty related to the data-MC difference for the fakes.
The fake rate method is validated with MC and the only systematic uncertainty trying to address the possible data-MC differences or the validity of the method in data is the one derived from the difference between the normal and rotated methods.

If possible, it would be good to find a better way to assess systematic uncertanities related to the difference in fakes shapes and normalization for each category between data and MC. We think that in general, for most analyses affected by fakes, this is the most important contribution related to fakes.
Limiting for now the discussion to the difference between the normal and rotated methods, first of all, we would ecourage to keep as nominal the normal method because, as we discussed previously, we do not think that it is correct to use the results averaged with the rotated one, as the latter is not used and the biases of the method are bound to be different in the two cases.
When you carried out the test, you got slightly smaller uncertainty using our proposal which also has larger uncertainty but for us it is likely that this was mainly due to the fact that the nominal predicts less fakes at high Q2 than the average, making the analysis more sensitive and giving a better expected sensitivity based on the Asymov. This could easily be verified if you wanted, setting the uncertainty to 0 in both cases and only changing the fakes expectation.
Furthermore, we do not think it is sensible to have a single nuisance that allows to exactly predict the difference at high Q2 if one has enough statistics at low Q2.
We would prefer for example to calculate the ratio, fit it with a function with a reasonable number of parameters (probably about 4), and use the uncertaintes for each of the parameters, after diagonalizing the covariance matrix. This should be done for every used histogram and category migration should also be taken ito account. One recent example can be seen in the proposed method for the smoothing of efficiency scale factors for the Wmass analysis:
AN2020_012_v4, section 8.21, Statistical uncertainties for smoothed SF [1].
FInally, as we also discussed in the authors-ARC meeting, the category migration should be affected by all nuisances.
Best Regards
Daniele and Marco
[1] https://cms.cern.ch/iCMS/jsp/db_notes/noteInfo.jsp?cmsnoteid=CMS%20AN-2020/012
