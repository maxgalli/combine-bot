QUESTION (by astepenn @ 2023-03-27T19:37:08.602Z):
Dear experts,
We’re studying the behavior of a test datacard with only 2 processes (1 signal 1 background) and 2 bins, and observation is set to match to expected background by construct (the card is copied below).
imax 2  number of channels
jmax 1  number of processes -1
kmax *  number of nuisance parameters (sources of systematical uncertainties)
shapes * * FAKE
-------
bin                   bin1    bin2
observation           100   10
-------
bin                   bin1    bin2	 bin1    bin2
process               sig    sig        bkg     bkg
process               0      0              1	   1
rate                  50.0    0.01        100	   10
-------
testUnc rateParam * bkg 1.0
#testUnc lnN -  - 1.3 1.3

As can be seen, signal is almost entirely in bin1, and bin2 is effectively a control region.
We came to the following result of this simplified card test which we cannot explain.
If background is let to float, the S+B fit will be effectively constrained by the bin2.
We expect the background to obtain about 30% relative uncertainty, since bin2 has 10 events in it.
Indeed we obtain an s+b postfit uncertainty of 29% via fitdiagnostics tool.
The asymptotic limit comes out to be ~1.1.
However, if we set a lognormal uncertainty on the background (correlated in the 2 bins) to be equal to the ‘same’ 30%, the resulting asymptotic limit is 0.8.
Could you please suggest how this inconsistency in result limits can be interpreted ?
kind regards,
Anton

REPLY 4 (by nckw @ 2023-03-28T12:07:41.000Z):
Hi Anton,
For the remaining difference, the reason comes from the fact that the correct prior for an uncertainty of this type is the gmN not lnN.
If I remove your lnN line and replace it with
testUnc gmN 10 - 10
then I get an expected limit ~1.1 as in the first case.
The above line tells combine that it should include a gmN constrained parameter where the region it is modelling has 10 events and the scale factor for the background process in this region is 10 (i.e 10*10=100).
You can find more about this in the combine docs here : http://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part2/settinguptheanalysis/#a-simple-counting-experiment
Best,
Nick
