REPLY 2 (by amarini @ 2024-07-15T13:40:10.106Z):
Hi Peter,
you may have removed (masked=1) all (?) the signal contributions from the likelihood.  Thus, the limit is going to be much much weaker.
Channel masking is used to NOT account for particular channels in the fitting procedure by removing their contribution to the (neg-log) likelihood function.
For many procedure, this is equivalent to removing those channels from the datacards directly.
(in your case, ~ combineCards.py --xc ‘sb_[0-4]’, tbc regex/escape )
The usefulness is e.g. to have postfit plots w/o a region contributing to it.
Please, check the documentation page on channel masking HERE
Cheers,
-Andrea

REPLY 4 (by amarini @ 2024-07-15T21:31:33.659Z):
Hi,
It’s obscure to me what you want me to verify or what you would like to achieve.
The two commands will likely yield different results.  I would recommend you to stick with --run blind or --run expect or run the std observed on the full dc, depending on the status of your analysis.
The first command will run the prefit (blind) Asymptotic upper limits on the cards without the sidebands (guessing by the name of your dc).
Your second command will fit something in some sideband (provided that sb are all and only the signal regions), but r.  The third one will run asymptotic limits on the result of the previous one (constructing an asimov dataset from there). Since you have some information from data, the two results will unlikely be identical.
If you want the postfit expected, just use --run expected.
-A
