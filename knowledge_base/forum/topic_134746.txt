REPLY 2 (by gortona @ 2025-10-20T09:39:23.196Z):
Dear Xingchen,
What is your background rate? My guess is that you are probably very close to some boundary. Do you allow r to go to negative values, or is it bounded to r>0? If you only allow for positive values, you nuisances would be expected to have more freedom to pull to higher values of r than to smaller ones.
I suspect from the second test you quote that there might also be some small bias in the PDF model, as for example your best fit does not converge exactly at 1.
Can you please share with us the workspaces you are using? It is difficult to provide more insights without seeing the workspaces.
Best,
Giacomo

REPLY 3 [ACCEPTED ANSWER] (by fanx @ 2025-10-20T17:24:29.826Z):
Hi Giacomo,
Thank you for your reply! I seemed to find the answer to my question here.
It is a known issue that posted here. When the systematics has too small impact, there would be an offset due to the numerical precision in the minimization. Maybe developers of combine can improve this in some future version. But this is really a small thing.
To reply to your questions, yes, r is allowed to be negative. And I don’t think the second test is subject to bias due to the profile choosing a different model. I also tried to fix the index, and I got the same result.
Best
Xingchen
