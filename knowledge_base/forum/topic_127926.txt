REPLY 2 (by kcormier @ 2025-07-31T07:06:25.039Z):
Hi Beren,
Roughly speaking the saturated GoF test tends to give expected distributions which look like \chi^2(n_d) where n_d is the number of bins in the analysis minus the number of free parameters. This is just a rough guide for your intuition, that’s the reason we perform the toys to estimate the distribution rather than performing some analytic calculation. But in terms of guiding your intuition it can be helpful.
From what I see in your analysis after a quick look at your data card and root file, I think you are only covering 2 data bins with this test? So the naive expectation would be a chi-squared with  1 d.o.f. which is indeed peaked at 0. Judging based on the reported p-value you show and where I see the blue arrow for the observed point, it looks like the distribution is somewhat wider than a \chi^2 with 1 degree of freedom, but as I said this is a naive estimate, so I don’t think there’s anything to be alarmed about.
in short: I didn’t check the rest of your setup/commands in any detail, but the expected GoF curve you show is consistent with my naive expectations due to it being a fit with 2 bins.
