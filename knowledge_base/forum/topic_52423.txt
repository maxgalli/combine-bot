REPLY 2 (by pkeicher @ 2024-10-10T08:32:02.041Z):
Hi Ying,
The error you see can only occur when you calculate uncertainties for the parameters. Did you also do this for the MultiDimFit?
In any case, the internal workings of MultiDimFit and FitDiagnostics are the same for the most part. However, there are still some subtle differences that can lead to a successful calculation of the errors in one method and a missing uncertainty in the other. Can you share the commands you used? If you are using the --robustFit method, the --stepSize option might help you find the crossing.
As a side note: you can also generate the prefit and postfit distributions without FitDiagnostics using the PostFitShapesFromWorkspace script that ships with the CombineHarvester, see docs here. That way, you don’t have to run the fit twice - you can save the fit result in your MultiDimFit with the option --saveFitResult and feed that into the script above.
Best,
Philip

Hi Ying,
thanks for the additional information. Just a couple of comments from my side:
The uncertainties on the shapes are indeed similar. It might be worthwhile to increase the number of sampled toys - that should make the numbers increasingly similar, so it’s a good cross check to ensure that you have full control.
I’m not sure if I understand what you mean here:

But from the number, it seems that the correlation between sysmatics in bins and channels is not added. I would like to know whether the uncertainty of correlation between systematics can also be added to histograms when run PostFitShapesFromWorkspace.

The uncertainty in the different bins is calculated by sampling nuisance parameter values from the covariance matrix that you provide the script with by passing e.g. the fit_mdf object. That means the procedure considers the correlations of all uncertainties that you consider in the fit. Note that in previous versions, you had to provide the --sampling option to activate this behavior, but that has been changed some while ago I think. I’m not entirely how it’s done internally in FitDiagnostics right now, maybe someone else can comment here.
Regarding the segmentation violation, I’ll try to follow up on that hopefully soon. Thanks for providing the output!
Cheers,

REPLY 9 (by pkeicher @ 2024-10-11T08:52:00.101Z):
Hi Ying,
thanks for the follow up. I’ll have to check the code of the PostFitShapesFromWorkspace script again, but indeed iirc, the total covariance matrix for all channels and processes is not considered by default. The correlation between processes is correctly taken into acount whenever you sum different processes together at generation time, which means that the Total* templates should have the correct uncertainties in your case.
There are two options that might be interesting to you though: --total-shapes generates shapes for the total sum of all channels, so you would also consider the correlations between the different processes across channels correctly. Additionally, there is the --covariance option, which also generates the covariance matrix you posted above.
This is all of course just to give you more information. If you’re happy with FitDiagnostics and can ensure that the uncertainties are calculated correctly, that’s of course also more than fine!
Cheers,
Philip
