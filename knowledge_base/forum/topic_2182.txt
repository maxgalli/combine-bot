QUESTION (by maglowac @ 2021-10-14T07:45:41.000Z):
*** Discussion title: Higgs Combination
Hi Combine experts,
this is more of a general query, we're open to ideas / suggestions.
I work on a Higgs to invisible analysis in the ttH and VH channels. Our
analysis is binned in terms of MET and jet multiplicities, to overcome
statistical limitations in certain bins we integrate over the number of
jets to leave ourselves with only a MET dependent rate parameter.
We would like to study the fit per individual category (MET & Jet mult)
but this is proving difficult due to the shared rate parameter between
categories.
For example; our rate parameter is computed from the combination of
categories: ttH5j1b, ttH5j2b, ttH6j1b, ttH6j2b (aka one rate param per
"super-category") is it possible to now study the individual category
(e.g. ttH5j1b) and somehow reference the shared rate parameter computed
from the "super-category"?
Many thanks, Maciek
----------------------------------------

REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2021-10-15T09:56:43.000Z):
*** Discussion title: Higgs Combination
Dear Maciek,
I think it depends on exactly what you want to study - if you are
interested in the interplay of the rateParam with the other parameters
in your category then I can't think of a good way of doing this test.
One thing that you can do is to take the value of the shared rateParam
from the full combined fit, and use --setParameters to set the value of
the rateParam to that value, and --freezeParameters to freeze it, when
you do a fit just of the ttH5j1b category. Whether this makes sense to
do probably depends on how strongly the rateParam is constrained by the
ttH5j1b category itself - I think if most of the constraint on the
rateParam comes from the other 3 categories then it is not unreasonable
to follow this procedure.
Best,
Adinda
