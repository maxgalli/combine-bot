QUESTION (by ineuteli @ 2022-06-23T17:08:46.846Z):
Dear Combine Tool experts,
I would like to compute the p-value or significance directly for the combined fit and for each individual fit performed by combine -M ChannelCompatibilityCheck, without redoing it separately for each channel.
With --saveFitResult, the ChannelCompatibilityCheck output already contains two RooFitResult objects, from which one can grab the signal strengths. Is there a way to use these results or perhaps a workspace snapshot to directly compute the corresponding p-value?
Otherwise, could I privately edit the combine tool source code, and add some lines to ChannelCompatibilityCheck::runSpecific that compute the NLL for the B-only fit and compares it to the NLL for each channel fit to obtain the significance as sqrt(2*q0)?
Or perhaps, I copy something like this routine in Significance::runSignificance for combine -M Significance:
ProfiledLikelihoodTestStatOpt testStat(*mc_s->GetObservables(), *mc_s->GetPdf(), mc_s->GetNuisanceParameters(),
nullParamValues, *mc_s->GetParametersOfInterest(), ...);
Double_t q0 = testStat.Evaluate(data, nullParamValues);
limit = (q0 > 0 ? sqrt(2*q0) : (uncapped_ ? -sqrt(-2*q0) : 0));

and evaluate it for each channel fit.

REPLY 2 (by agilbert @ 2022-06-27T11:44:08.543Z):
Hi Izaak,
The easiest way to proceed is to probably do the mapping of POIs to channels via a physics model, instead of relying on the ChannelCompatibilityCheck mode. This way, you could use any of the standard methods (Significance, MultiDimFit) to extract the information you need. The MultiSignalModel could be a straightforward way to do this: https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part2/physicsmodels/#multisignalmodel-ready-made-model-for-multiple-signal-processes
Would this work in your case?
cheers,
