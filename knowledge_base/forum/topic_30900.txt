Dear experts,
Bob Cousins pointed me to the paper

arxiv.org

2109.13426.pdf

which has extended the case considered by Chernoff for two signal strengths with mu_1 > 0, mu_2 > 0 condition where p-value is calculated as:
pvalue = 0.5TMath::Prob(2deltaNLL,1)+0.25TMath::Prob(2deltaNLL,2);
to the n signal strengths with mu_i > 0 (i = 1, n).
In our first publication (HIG-16-017) in agreement with Stat.Com. and conveners we combined two Search Regions, SR1 and SR2 for 8 TeV data using Chernoff formula for the calulation of the local significance.  The reason was that we did not have TH model which can relate SR1 and SR2.
Nich Wardle and Andre David have kindly checked Chernoff formula with toy experiments
In this iteration of the analysis we have three Search Regions, SR1, SR2, SR3 and two years, 2017 and 2018 to be combined and we do not have TH signal model. So, we do not have signal MC which can relate SRs with different topologes and also relate 2017 and 2018 years with different running conditions.
In this situation my proposal is to follow what we did in the first paper.  For  combining of 2017 and 2018 data withing a given SR to calculate local significance in this SR we use again Chernoff formula.
When we combined three SRs and two years we used extended formula with n = 6.
Any feedback and discussion are very welcome.
Cheers, Sasha.

REPLY 2 (by nckw @ 2023-10-24T09:43:35.000Z):
Thanks Sasha,
This is the discussion I couldn’t recall but indeed the result is the one you pointed to in the paper from Fowlie. We should be careful in that this result assumes that any of the signals being +ve adds to the test statistic. The other way one could extend the 1D case is that any -ve signal results in a test-stat of 0 and the p-value calculation would be different. This other way is not very useful since of course we’re interested in any of the bumps being +ve, so I think using the formulae from Fowlie is the right way to go here – thanks for digging out the history and thanks Bob for the pointer to the Fowlie paper.
Now for the specific case here, I agree that for 8TeV data, we don’t have a TH model to relate to the 13 TeV SRs but surely our CMS simulation can account for differences in running conditions. So I would have thought it made more sense to have a separate signals for 8TeV and 13 TeV but not for every region separately? This probably is something more that should be discussed in the SUS PAG and with the statistics committee of course,
Best,
Nick

REPLY 3 (by anikiten @ 2023-10-24T10:19:11.077Z):
Hi Nick,
thanks for replay.
for the first paper we actually combined SR1 and SR2 for 8 TeV with Chernoff formula, but we did not make an attempt for combine 8 and 13 (2016) data, since we have observed no signal with 13 TeV (2016) data.  We did a such combination of SR1 and SR2 at 8 TeV with Chernoff since we do not have TH model relating efficiencies of the signal selection in SR1 and SR2 for a given LHC energy. So we assumed the observation of two bumps at 8 TeV in SR1 and SR2 as independent.
Now SUS conveners want to have first, combination of 2017 and 2018 in separately SR1 and SR2 and SR3.   So, my first problem is to combine 2017 and 2018 data with different running conditions and without having the signal model (MC). So, my very first thought was to assume conservatively 2017 and 2018 measurement of the bumps for a given SR as independent. It is conservative approach I think which should give smaller significance compared with one signal strength only provided that ratio the signal efficiency in 2017 and 2018 is known (which is ot the case).
Anyway, it is a good idea to discuss it in Stat.Com and with SUS conveners and PC.
Cheers, Sasha.
