QUESTION (by eipearso @ 2025-08-01T16:27:40.761Z):
Dear combine experts,
We are in the process of unblinding our analysis and have been asked to make  pre and post background-only fit plots. We have been able to obtain the pre and post b-only fit yields with FitDiagnostics as detailed in [1] with the following:
combine -M FitDiagnostics datacard.txt -m 1500 --plots --robustFit 1 -v2 --rMin -1 --rMax 3 --saveShapes --saveWithUncertainties --skipSBFit

The workspace and datacard are attached [2, 3].
However, we find that the uncertainties we obtain are the total (stat+syst) uncertainty. We would like to report the yield for each background process with the statistical and systematic uncertainties separately. There is a “Breakdown of uncertainties” section of the docs [4], but this uses MultiDimFit instead of FitDiagnostics and obtains the breakdown of uncertainties for the best-fit r value. How do we obtain post-fit yields with the statistic and systematic uncertainties separate?
Note: We did try running FitDiagnostics with groups of nuisances frozen, but obtained results that were not self consistent. We ran the above command three times: once with all nuisances floating (to get total uncertainty), once with the statistical nuisances frozen (to get the systematic uncertainty), and once with the systematic nuisances frozen (to get the statistical uncertainty). However, when obtained in this way the sum in quadrature of the stat and syst uncertainties does not equal the total uncertainty.
Thank you,
Emma Pearson

REPLY 3 (by amarini @ 2025-08-01T21:09:50.601Z):
Hi,
Please note (as Giacomo said) this:
While the above procedure is guaranteed the have the effect that the sum in quadrature of the breakdown will equal the total uncertainty, the order in which you freeze the groups can make a difference due to correlations induced by the fit. You should check if the answers change significantly if changing the order and we recommend you start with the largest group (in terms of overall contribution to the uncertainty) first, working down the list in order of the size of the contribution.

On top of this, if you want to do it for postfit uncertainties, make sure you start from the best fit and not from the prefit, similarly to what done in https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/nonstandard/#breakdown-of-uncertainties
Otherwise, you will get uncertainties that do not refer to the same fit.
-Andrea

REPLY 5 (by eipearso @ 2025-09-17T16:28:45.026Z):
Dear combine experts,
We thought we had figured this out, but we found that it does not work consistently for all of our mass points. The sequence of commands is as follows:
(1) Run a b-only MultiDimFit with all nuisances floating and save the snapshot:
combine -M MultiDimFit [datacard] -m [mass] --rMin -1 --rMax 2 --robustFit 1 --saveWorkspace --setParameters r=0,signalScaleParam=[SF] --trackParameters signalScaleParam --freezeParameters signalScaleParam,r

(2) Run FitDiagnostics with all nuisances floating and load the snapshot from (1):
combine -M FitDiagnostics [multiDimFit snapshot] -m [mass] --rMin -1 --rMax 2 --robustFit 1 --skipSBFit --plots --saveShapes --saveNormalizations --saveWithUncertainties --saveOverallShapes --snapshotName MultiDimFit --setParameters signalScaleParam=[SF] --trackParameters signalScaleParam --freezeParameters signalScaleParam

(3) Run FitDiagnostics like in (2), but with the systematics frozen. (Note that we defined a “systs” group in our datacard to make this easier):
combine -M FitDiagnostics [multiDimFit snapshot] -m [mass] --rMin -1 --rMax 2 --robustFit 1 --skipSBFit --plots --saveShapes --saveNormalizations --saveWithUncertainties --saveOverallShapes --snapshotName MultiDimFit --setParameters signalScaleParam=[SF] --trackParameters signalScaleParam --freezeParameters signalScaleParam --freezeNuisanceGroup systs

For some mass points this works just fine, and we can obtain the uncertainties broken into stat and syst components. For others, it fails in step (3) and we see an error message: Error in <ROOT::Math:: Fitter::SetFCN>: FCN function has zero parameters . The difference appears to be the amount of statistics in the backgrounds relative to the autoMCStats threshold. The above set of commands fails in step (3) for mass points with enough statistics that all years are above the autoMCStats threshold.
Everything to reproduce this, including a txt file with combine commands with the mass and scale factors filled in, has been put here (https://cernbox.cern.ch/s/nSyDPmrVru78RDY) for mass 300 GeV (fails) and mass 1100 GeV (succeeds).
What do you suggest to do about the masses where this fails?
Thank you,
Em Pearson

REPLY 10 (by gortona @ 2025-09-29T20:47:26.000Z):
Dear Emma,
We discussed a bit about this with Massimiliano.
I don’t think there is an easy way out within fitDiagnostics to have a fit with no nuisance parameters and no POI. I can suggest you two possible solutions:
The first one is to rely on MultiDimFit instead of fitDiagnostics in the last step. Although this means you would not get the postFit distribution in the output file.
The other option is to add a negligible systematic uncertainty (like 0.1% or 0.01%) in the datacard and leave it floating. this should have no effect on the actual numbers you obtain but it introduces something for the fit to operate on.
I hope it helps,
Best regards,
Giacomo

Dear Emma,
We discussed a bit about this with Massimiliano.
I don’t think there is an easy way out within fitDiagnostics to have a fit with no nuisance parameters and no POI. I can suggest you two possible solutions:
The first one is to rely on MultiDimFit instead of fitDiagnostics in the last step. Although this means you would not get the postFit distribution in the output file.
The other option is to add a negligible systematic uncertainty (like 0.1% or 0.01%) in the datacard and leave it floating. this should have no effect on the actual numbers you obtain but it introduces something for the fit to operate on.
