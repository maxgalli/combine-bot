QUESTION (by ckapsiak @ 2026-05-04T21:00:21.562Z):
Hello Everyone,
We are working on a 2D bump hunt using a data-driven background estimation.
We are using Gaussian process regression for our background estimate, which outputs a MVN distribution.
To use this MVN estimate in Combine, we treat the mean of the MVN as the background estimate, and then do an spectral decomposition of the covariance matrix to produce “eigen-variations,” which are treated as the up/down variations of the nominal background.
One peculiarity we have encountered is that when there are smaller bin counts and/or large up/down variations, this eigen-decomposition can produce down shape variations which have negative count in some bins.
Currently, we have been “clipping” the down variations so they cannot become negative.
Our question is if this is the correct way to handle things.
If the goal it to replicate the MVN and to avoid bias, it seems like we want to have the real up/down variations without clipping, to that they can “interfere” as needed to emulate the MVN.
On the other hand, negative bin counts are obviously non-physical and we don’t want to make Combine upset.
Please let us know if you have insights into this matter.
Best,
Charlie and the Single Stop Team
