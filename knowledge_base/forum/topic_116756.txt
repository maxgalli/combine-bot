REPLY 5 [ACCEPTED ANSWER] (by agilbert @ 2025-02-25T17:39:00.657Z):
Hi,
To clarify, normal rateParams are not varied in the standard toy generation (without --toysFrequentist) because they have no associated constraint PDF, nor with --toysFrequentist, as there is no associated global observable parameter (but the value of the rateParams used for the toy generation will be adjusted by the initial fit to data).
When you come to fit each toy (with whichever method in combine you are using), the rateParam parameters are still floating in the fit, so we would expect to see some variation in their fitted values looking at the ensemble of toys.
The influence of --setParameters: this changes the values of the rateParams before the toy generation begins, thus you will see changes in the toys. This shouldn’t have any real effect with --toysFrequentist since the rateParams are going to be adjusted in the fit to data.
For --setParameterRanges this will only influence the allowed ranges during the fits to the toy data sets.
cheers,
Andrew

REPLY 7 (by agilbert @ 2025-02-26T09:39:36.736Z):
Hi Andrzej,
It’s not guaranteed that fluctuations on the fitted values of the rateParams will be small. For example, if there are large systematics affecting background normalisations in CRs, that are then varying a lot in the toys, you could see a larger spread on the rateParams to accommodate this.
