QUESTION (by geliu @ 2025-08-13T14:43:26.483Z):
Dear Experts,
I am writing to consult about an issue we are faced with when computing the asymptotic limits for HIG-23-001, searching for heavy resonances decaying to 2l2q. I tried my best but could not resolve it.
Let me narrate the issue step by step:

An example of the original datacard is in [1] on lxplus. The statistical model is 2-d with a mass variable zz2lJ_mass and a kinematic discriminant Dspin0. There are two signals (ggH_hzz, qqH_hzz) constructed as RooDoubleCB(zz2lJ_mass)*RooHistPdf(Dspin0 | zz2lJ_mass), and three backgrounds constructed as RooHistPdf(zz2lJ_mass)*RooHistPdf(Dspin0 | zz2lJ_mass). You can see that the mass of the signals are parameterized, while that of backgrounds is described by histograms. This datacard works fine (as far as we understood).
Based on some comments, we plan to parameterize the background mass shapes, i.e. replace their RooHistPdf(zz2lJ_mass) by a parameterized Pdf. The Pdf form we chose is RooggZZPdf_v2. The new datacard can be found in [2].
However, the new version doesn’t give a reasonable result. In both [1] and [2], you can find a log file with blinded asymptotic limits, freezing all nuisance parameters. And there is a big difference between them, which is not expected.
I am wondering what is the reason for the difference. I checked all Pdf distributions as well as normalizations, but I don’t see any evident difference between the two datacards. Therefore I am not sure if it is a subtle issue in combine.

Really appreciate it if you could help us figure this out!
Thanks very much!
Yours Sincerely,
Geliang

REPLY 6 (by gortona @ 2025-08-22T15:19:23.000Z):
Dear Geliang,
It is a subtle question. In principle, what matters to define the PDF is the input histogram, not the RooRealVar binning. But the problem is that for several operations, including integrations or toy sampling, RooFit will rely on the binning defined by the x variable. I think that in unbinned datasets this probably won’t matter as it should be recognised as such, but in binned dataset it might cause troubles, especially if the bin edges don’t match. I remember this being a problem in the past in HZZ, which uses a very similar setup to your analysis.
As a general rule, it is way safer to set the zz2lJ_mass binning exactly matching the binning of the input histogram and not having to worry about this anymore
Cheers,
Giacomo
