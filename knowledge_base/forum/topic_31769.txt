Dear Data Analysis friends,
The next HSF Data Analysis WG meeting is dedicated to the topic of the HEP Statistics Serialization Standard (HS3).
It takes place on Monday, Nov 27 at 16:00 CET
https://indico.cern.ch/event/1348309
with Zoom connection details provided on the agenda.
The HS3 project defines a standard for representing statistical models in a form that is both human- and machine-readable. Check out the GitHub repository [1] for more details! A common serialization format allows for the interoperability of statistical tools and helps streamline the publishing of statistical models.
We would like to use this meeting to showcase the ongoing work to the DAWG community and also invite feedback about HS3.
We are looking forward to seeing you there,
Nicole, Nick, Alex
[1] https://github.com/hep-statistics-serialization-standard/hep-statistics-serialization-standard

REPLY 4 (by kcormier @ 2023-11-17T16:20:40.457Z):
Hi Maurizio,
The choice of the forum was made by those leading the HS3 effort as they are trying to make more people aware of the effort.
We have not had much more engagement with HS3 than what was done in the spring and early summer with a few meetings/discussions. The only activity really is having set up a contact person for HS3 within CMS, and he’s had a first e-mail exchange with Carsten (leading the HS3 effort) where carsten provided some basic info about the status of the effort.
But a major reason for our lack of involvement is the general lack of movement or discussion. As far as we know this is the first real meeting or discussion on the issue since the summer. At that point they decided that meetings were not as effective as they’d like and wanted to move the discussion more onto github [1]. There is some activity there, though not much.
Perhaps @ncsmith can comment if I’ve missed something, as I know he has been somewhat active in the discussions. He already did.
