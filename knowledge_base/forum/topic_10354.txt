REPLY 2 (by amarini @ 2022-05-11T19:05:39.092Z):
Hi Lata,
I didn’t go through the detail of your analysis or your implementation.
From a first glance from your slides, it seems you are using parametric shapes (eg Gaussian, or CB).
Usually, in CMS we interpolate the parameters and normalization of these functions (w/ RooSpline1D [*]), rather than using an integral morphing technique, since this prove to be faster (you don’t need to invert the CDF every time) and more stable. For example, Hgg always persued this path.
For instance:

you fit your model (eg gaus) for your mass points. Eg 500,600,700. You save somewhere mean, sigma, norm (…)
you construct the splines out of the ones above mean (MH) , sigma (MH), norm (MH), (…)
you construct your final model (Gaussian) with the spline above and put the norm in the workspace. (as final model name +  _norm)

-Andrea
[*] in combine, you may need to load the library.
