REPLY 2 (by andrea_marini @ 2021-08-18T18:53:32.000Z):
Hi Hugo,
In general, the W asymmetry behaves as a ratio of observables, so common correlated uncertainties usually simplify,
while anticorrelated uncertainties are counted more.
In general, it would be wise to make sure that the correlation scheme of the uncertainties is correct (correlated, uncorrelated, vs anticorrelated).
Probably you would benefit in using finer splitting for jes, jer, pdf, xsec … as the residuals in each of them will behave differently in the W-asymmetry measurement.
For example, for the jet energy scale, you should understand if the sources give you a larger uncertainty when propagated to the W-asymm.
Similarly, the jer. For the Pdf you can use the hessian eigenvalues (if available) that represents the different uncorrelated components. For the xsec, you can try to split the two scales or through the different kinematic regions the calculation may be sensitive to, due to different effects (but this may require some inputs from the theorists).
Cheers,
-Andrea
