REPLY 4 (by vischia @ 2023-02-13T17:18:48.000Z):
Hi Anton,
if you want to let the background float but not interpret its normalization as a cross section measurement, then the rateParam is fine.
If you want to do a simultaneous determination of the signal and background cross section, then you should look into the multisignal models, which in practice means you should declare two POIs, one scaling the signal and one scaling the background (e.g. instead of r you would get r_signal and r_background).
Cheers,
Pietro

REPLY 5 (by adewit @ 2023-02-13T17:29:32.905Z):
Hi Anton,
You asked how to output the normalization of the background after a fit: you can get this from the shapes (and consequently normalizations) saved when you run FitDiagnostics with --saveShapes. These will include the effects of all systematic uncertainties and so provide the post-fit signal and background rates. This is strictly different from adding a rateParam, which does not directly correspond to the total background rate after the fit in the presence of other systematic uncertainties. However, if you have a rateParam in your model and you want to see its value, you can use the option --trackParameters, you can search for it in this section: https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/runningthetool/#common-command-line-options
Best,
Adinda
