REPLY 2 (by amarini @ 2022-08-01T22:33:28.729Z):
Hi, I guess that you are asking about the ‘saturated’ likelihood test statistics.
It is also called Generalized chi-square goodness of fit link; under certain condition it can be considered a chi-square. However the proper distribution accounting for the effect of the different systematics should be derived with toys. For more details see the link above.
The other methods implemented are the Kolmogorov–Smirnov and the Anderson–Darling tests.They share a similar concern about the systematic effects, but furethermore these two tests are inherently defined over a 1D distribution, so some assumptions have been made for multichannel experiments (as the sum over the channels, iirc).
In conclusion, you should always run toys to determine the test-statistics distribution.
