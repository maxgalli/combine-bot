REPLY 2 (by amarini @ 2024-02-25T21:07:12.177Z):
Hi Sasha,
The goodness of fit method will compute the value of a test statistic of your choice.
E.g., --algo saturated will compute the saturated likelihood test statistics, which for likelihood models behaves very similarly to a chi2 [*].
In order to get a p-value, you need to know both the value of the observed test statistics, and its expected distribution. The latter can be obtained with toys.
The goodness of fit result (p-value) will check how frequent it is in the toys to obtain a worse value of the test-statistic distribution.
In the online guide there are already scripts that will plot the distribution and print the p-value.
Please, note that by default the signal strength is left floating in the fit, so that the measure is independent from the presence or absence of a signal. It is also recommended to use the frequentist approach in toys generation (--toysFreq) for saturated models.
You can refer to https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/commonstatsmethods/#goodness-of-fit-tests for further information.
-A
[*] https://www.physics.ucla.edu/~cousins/stats/cousins_saturated.pdf
