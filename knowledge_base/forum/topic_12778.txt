Dear experts,
I’m studying the measurement of asymmetry of a particular angular distribution (denoted by cos θ^N) [1] with the Higgs combine tool, and currently, I’m looking at the impacts of JES uncertainties.
I made two processes; one has “total” JES uncertainty, and the other has uncertainties from individual JES sources [2], [3]. I put JES uncertainties by drawing histograms with varied jet pT* and inserting them into the Higgs combine tool, and calculated the pulls and impacts with the Asimov dataset. And then, I obtained these plots. (The asymmetry is set as the POI.)

I expected that “Total” JES uncertainty gives a larger impact than each of the individual JES sources. But in this result, many sources of JES have much larger impacts than the “Total” JES, even considering its constraint. I’m not sure whether or not it can happen… Or, is there any similar case, I mean, any other case with a larger impact by individual JES sources than the “total” JES?

REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2022-07-12T20:36:52.009Z):
Dear Byeonghak Ko,
Yes, what you are observing can certainly happen. The assumption that the impact of a total uncertainty should be larger than that of individual sources corresponding to the same uncertainty is probably roughly right for pure normalisation effects, but no longer holds for uncertainties with a shape effect. What matters in that case is how much the total uncertainty and the individual sources mimic the signal. To take the example you give here: if the effect of shifting JES TimePtEta by ±1sigma on the background model (with AFB=0) “looks like” a signal+background model (ie for some nonzero AFB value), but the same ±1sigma shift for the total JES does not mimic the signal+background model,  then I think it is expected that the impact of JES TimePtEta is larger than the impact of the total JES.
Hope this helps,
Adinda
