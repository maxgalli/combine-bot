REPLY 8 (by kcormier @ 2023-07-18T11:32:45.440Z):
Hi Jesse,
I just wanted to clarify one point.
The naive expectation of sqrt(2) you are referring to is the naive expectation for combining two independent measurements of the same quantity where the two combinations have the same statistics, and assuming the measurements are statistically limited.  But by “independent” here, what is meant is that the events contributing to each analysis are independent.
This is not the expectation for making a joint measurement of two independently distributed observables. Where here, “independently distributed” means that the distributions of each observable factorizes. i.e. p(x,y) = p(x)*p(y). I believe that this is the situation you were trying to test, but note that there is no simple sqrt(2) naive expectation in this case, it depends on the distributions.
Consider a toy example:
100 background events, uniformly distributed in some observable O1, which is split into ten bins, such that the expectation in each bin is 10 events. Assume the signal expectation is entirely concentrated in one bin of the distribution.
What happens if you do a joint measurement of this observable with another independent observable, O2, whose expectations are the same? Instead of a your signal being in a single bin with 10 expected background events, those ten background events are evenly distributed across the second observable, and you expect only a single background event, whereas your signal in that bin is not diluted at all.
From this toy example, I hope you can see that the expected sensitivity that comes from a join measurement of two independently distributed observables does not follow a simple sqrt(2) scaling, which is for a rather different situation of two independent measurement of the same quantity with the same total statistical power. In general, the improvement will depend on the distributions you are considering.
The github is this one: https://github.com/cms-analysis/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/tree/main , which Aliya posted. Feel free to ask any follow up questions if something there isn’t clear however.
Hope that helps,
Cheers,
Kyle
