I am performing S+B fit with a function defined as a pdf. The limits are calculated by Asymptotic method with an additional option --saveworkspace also given. I can see the background distribution from the Roodatasets saved in the workspace. But I want to see how the S+B fit look like for the different bins of the input mass histogram.

REPLY 2 (by amarini @ 2022-05-24T09:50:09.446Z):
Hi Jyoti,
combine -M MultiDimFit --saveWorkspace saves the workspace used by combine and a snapshot of the S+B background with name MultiDimFit
If you want to visualize the distributions,
combine -M FitDiagnostics --saveShapes save as TH1 the prefit, b-only, and s+b shapes.  If you want also unceratinties on the fits, you can use additionally the option --saveWithUncertainties, which is slow, but resamples the covariance matrix.
Cheers,
-Andrea

REPLY 4 (by amarini @ 2022-05-24T10:41:37.000Z):
Hi,
Yes, shapes_prefit has the histogram as you put them in your datacards. total corresponds to the S+B, total_background to the sum of all backgrounds and total_signal to the sum of all signals.
