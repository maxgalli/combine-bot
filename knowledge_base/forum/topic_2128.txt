REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2021-07-26T15:54:21.000Z):
*** Discussion title: Higgs Combination
Hi Saswati,
It looks like the issue is related to the usage of RobustHesse to get a
positive-definite covariance matrix; which in this case is dropping a
few of the nuisance parameters to accomplish this. The covariance matrix
resampling step that is triggered when you run with --saveShapes
--saveWithUncertainties appears to set the dropped parameter(s) to NaN,
giving you these errors. We don't have a simple fix within
FitDiagnostics for this, but what you can do is run FitDiagnostics
*without* --saveShapes --saveWithUncertainties, and produce the pre- and
post-fit shapes with PostFitShapesFromWorkspace [*], a tool available in
CombineHarvester.
You will still need to run FitDiagnostics to produce the covariance
matrix. In the example in the documentation fit_s is passed, as you are
skipping the S+B fit you should pass fit_b (so -f
fitDiagnostics.root:fit_b) because it's the only one that exists.
Best,
Adinda
[*]
http://cms-analysis.github.io/CombineHarvester/post-fit-shapes-ws.html

REPLY 9 (by adewit @ 2021-08-11T13:44:43.000Z):
*** Discussion title: Higgs Combination
Hi Saswati,
Sorry for the delay - I still don't fully understand why in some cases
parameters that are dropped from the covariance matrix by robustHesse
don't cause issues in the production of the post-fit shapes +
uncertainties. One thing that works around the issue is to freeze, in
the PostFitShapesFromWorkspace call, the parameters that get dropped
(you can find it in the fit log) to be able to invert the Hessian. You
can do this by adding e.g. --freeze
QCDscale_ttbar,CMS_bbwwsl_thu_shape_DY to the PostFitShapesFromWorkspace
command.
Of course it would be even better to investigate why those parameters
are problematic, and resolve the issue, so that they don't need to be
removed for the Hessian to be invertible in the first place. Things you
could check are whether these parameters are strongly anti-correlated
with other parameters of the fit, or whether they have a highly
asymmetric effect on the processes they act on. There is unfortunately
no guaranteed set of steps that will lead to the solution, however.
Best,
Adinda
