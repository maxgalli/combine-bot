REPLY 2 (by nckw @ 2026-03-09T20:33:35.000Z):
Hi Giorgos,
I don’t think there’s any issues here, everything is working as expected.
The reason why some pulls are not calculated is that the definition of the pull used for the blue markers involves a denominator that has a difference between the pre and post fit uncertainty (see the definition here https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/latest/part3/nonstandard/#nuisance-parameter-impacts). In the case where this difference is zero, the pull is not defined and so the tool simply leaves it off. This seems to be the case here.
Best
Nick

REPLY 5 (by nckw @ 2026-03-10T10:18:27.586Z):
Hi Giorgos,
Yes indeed, that’s the paper where the definition comes from. You’re right in that sometimes we can get these cases where the denominator is 0 or even a sqrt of a -ve although in the latter case, this says that the uncertainty after looking at the data is larger than the uncertainty before. This is clearly pointing to something strange unless its really just they are ~ equal within the noise of the numerical precision of our fits.
There are other definitions of the pull, which we have implemented in combine (you can see here : https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/latest/part3/nonstandard/#pulls), but I admit that I am not sure if this particular tool has an option to plot them as the blue markers. Perhaps you can just calculate them using the other tool and check those for which the pull definition you have currently isn’t applicable.
Best,
Nick
