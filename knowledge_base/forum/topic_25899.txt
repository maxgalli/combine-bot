REPLY 2 (by kcormier @ 2023-06-27T13:47:42.365Z):
Hi Stephanie,
Some first thoughts:
One difference between the two fits, in the first case (the MultiDimFit run with --algo none which is the default), the MultiDim fit is not reporting the uncertainties directly, its just doing the minimization.
By using -v 3 you’ve been able to check the uncertainties on the parameters.  Unless I’m mistaken, these will be the uncertainties reported from MINUIT’s minimization step, from its error matrix, and hence are reported as symmetric intervals.
When running the impacts, the asymmetric intervals (using MINOS rather than the symmetric ones from the error matrix) are computed and then the impact on the POI at each of the 1 sigma bounds is determined at these points.
In the original (--algo none, default MultiDimFit) fit, its possible the fit never goes anywhere near these +/-1 sigma points. So it could be that things appear reasonably well-behaved when performing a the minimzation and taking the error matrix from the second derivative at the minimum, but that there are issues elsewhere in the fit.
As a first step I would suggest maybe running a scan of the NLL over the parameter(s) that are giving you trouble. [1]
e.g.
combine -M MultiDimFit --algo grid --points 100 -d <your_datacard> --P p1BTag
plot1DScan.py higgsCombineTest.MultiDimFit.mH120.root --POI p1BTag

with other customization options as necessary.
That will produce a 1D scan of the NLL for that parameter. It may show a large asymmetry, or some other pathology (such as a kink in the NLL) which may serve as a starting point to investigate what is going on, or what next steps might be taken.
As an aside, it’s not clear to me why in the original fit you are redefining all of your nuisance parameters as POIs? It may not be relevant to the technical issue you’re asking about, given what you’ve said; but in general this is changing the fit model, and typically not what would be appropriate from a physics point of view, and lead to different results. Of course it depends what you’re looking to do, which I don’t know.
I hope this helps,
Kyle
[1] https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/commonstatsmethods/#likelihood-fits-and-scans

Hi Stephanie,
Some first thoughts:
One difference between the two fits, in the first case (the MultiDimFit run with --algo none which is the default), the MultiDim fit is not reporting the uncertainties directly, its just doing the minimization.
By using -v 3 you’ve been able to check the uncertainties on the parameters. Unless I’m mistaken, these will be the uncertainties reported from MINUIT’s minimization step, from its error matrix, and hence are reported as symmetric intervals.
When running the impacts, the asymmetric intervals (using MINOS rather than the symmetric ones from the error matrix) are computed and then the impact on the POI at each of the 1 sigma bounds is determined at these points.
In the original (–algo none, default MultiDimFit) fit, its possible the fit never goes anywhere near these +/-1 sigma points. So it could be that things appear reasonably well-behaved when performing a the minimzation and taking the error matrix from the second derivative at the minimum, but that there are issues elsewhere in the fit.
As a first step I would suggest maybe running a scan of the NLL over the parameter(s) that are giving you trouble. [1]
e.g.
combine -M MultiDimFit --algo grid --points 100 -d <your_datacard> --P p1BTag
plot1DScan.py higgsCombineTest.MultiDimFit.mH120.root --POI p1BTag
with other customization options as necessary.
That will produce a 1D scan of the NLL for that parameter. It may show a large asymmetry, or some other pathology (such as a kink in the NLL) which may serve as a starting point to investigate what is going on, or what next steps might be taken.
As an aside, it’s not clear to me why in the original fit you are redefining all of your nuisance parameters as POIs? It may not be relevant to the technical issue you’re asking about, given what you’ve said; but in general this is changing the fit model, and typically not what would be appropriate from a physics point of view, and lead to different results. Of course it depends what you’re looking to do, which I don’t know.
I hope this helps,
Kyle
[1] https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/commonstatsmethods/#likelihood-fits-and-scans

Have you tried playing with the tolerances? Add these things to your combine command:
–cminDefaultMinimizerTolerance {tolerance} --robustFit 1 --setRobustFitTolerance {tolerance} --stepSize {tolerance} --setCrossingTolerance {tolerance}
{tolerance} is some small number, they do not have to be the same numbers for each. A good idea is to have them be half expected size of the impact.
They make the fit slower at small values, so but for this specific issue this should be fine.

Hi Stephanie,
Some first thoughts:
One difference between the two fits, in the first case (the MultiDimFit run with --algo none which is the default), the MultiDim fit is not reporting the uncertainties directly, its just doing the minimization.
By using -v 3 you’ve been able to check the uncertainties on the parameters. Unless I’m mistaken, these will be the uncertainties reported from MINUIT’s minimization step, from its error matrix, and hence are reported as symmetric intervals.
When running the impacts, the asymmetric intervals (using MINOS rather than the symmetric ones from the error matrix) are computed and then the impact on the POI at each of the 1 sigma bounds is determined at these points.
In the original (–algo none, default MultiDimFit) fit, its possible the fit never goes anywhere near these +/-1 sigma points. So it could be that things appear reasonably well-behaved when performing a the minimzation and taking the error matrix from the second derivative at the minimum, but that there are issues elsewhere in the fit.
As a first step I would suggest maybe running a scan of the NLL over the parameter(s) that are giving you trouble. [1]
e.g.
combine -M MultiDimFit --algo grid --points 100 -d <your_datacard> --P p1BTag
plot1DScan.py higgsCombineTest.MultiDimFit.mH120.root --POI p1BTag
with other customization options as necessary.
That will produce a 1D scan of the NLL for that parameter. It may show a large asymmetry, or some other pathology (such as a kink in the NLL) which may serve as a starting point to investigate what is going on, or what next steps might be taken.
As an aside, it’s not clear to me why in the original fit you are redefining all of your nuisance parameters as POIs? It may not be relevant to the technical issue you’re asking about, given what you’ve said; but in general this is changing the fit model, and typically not what would be appropriate from a physics point of view, and lead to different results. Of course it depends what you’re looking to do, which I don’t know.
I hope this helps,
Kyle

Hi Stephanie,
Some first thoughts:
One difference between the two fits, in the first case (the MultiDimFit run with --algo none which is the default), the MultiDim fit is not reporting the uncertainties directly, its just doing the minimization.
By using -v 3 you’ve been able to check the uncertainties on the parameters. Unless I’m mistaken, these will be the uncertainties reported from MINUIT’s minimization step, from its error matrix, and hence are reported as symmetric intervals.
When running the impacts, the asymmetric intervals (using MINOS rather than the symmetric ones from the error matrix) are computed and then the impact on the POI at each of the 1 sigma bounds is determined at these points.
In the original (–algo none, default MultiDimFit) fit, its possible the fit never goes anywhere near these +/-1 sigma points. So it could be that things appear reasonably well-behaved when performing a the minimzation and taking the error matrix from the second derivative at the minimum, but that there are issues elsewhere in the fit.
As a first step I would suggest maybe running a scan of the NLL over the parameter(s) that are giving you trouble. [1]
e.g.
combine -M MultiDimFit --algo grid --points 100 -d <your_datacard> --P p1BTag
plot1DScan.py higgsCombineTest.MultiDimFit.mH120.root --POI p1BTag
with other customization options as necessary.
That will produce a 1D scan of the NLL for that parameter. It may show a large asymmetry, or some other pathology (such as a kink in the NLL) which may serve as a starting point to investigate what is going on, or what next steps might be taken.
As an aside, it’s not clear to me why in the original fit you are redefining all of your nuisance parameters as POIs? It may not be relevant to the technical issue you’re asking about, given what you’ve said; but in general this is changing the fit model, and typically not what would be appropriate from a physics point of view, and lead to different results. Of course it depends what you’re looking to do, which I don’t know.
I hope this helps,
Kyle

Hi Stephanie,
Some first thoughts:
One difference between the two fits, in the first case (the MultiDimFit run with --algo none which is the default), the MultiDim fit is not reporting the uncertainties directly, its just doing the minimization.
By using -v 3 you’ve been able to check the uncertainties on the parameters. Unless I’m mistaken, these will be the uncertainties reported from MINUIT’s minimization step, from its error matrix, and hence are reported as symmetric intervals.
When running the impacts, the asymmetric intervals (using MINOS rather than the symmetric ones from the error matrix) are computed and then the impact on the POI at each of the 1 sigma bounds is determined at these points.
In the original (--algo none, default MultiDimFit) fit, its possible the fit never goes anywhere near these +/-1 sigma points. So it could be that things appear reasonably well-behaved when performing a the minimzation and taking the error matrix from the second derivative at the minimum, but that there are issues elsewhere in the fit.
As a first step I would suggest maybe running a scan of the NLL over the parameter(s) that are giving you trouble. [1]
e.g.
combine -M MultiDimFit --algo grid --points 100 -d <your_datacard> --P p1BTag
plot1DScan.py higgsCombineTest.MultiDimFit.mH120.root --POI p1BTag

with other customization options as necessary.
That will produce a 1D scan of the NLL for that parameter. It may show a large asymmetry, or some other pathology (such as a kink in the NLL) which may serve as a starting point to investigate what is going on, or what next steps might be taken.
As an aside, it’s not clear to me why in the original fit you are redefining all of your nuisance parameters as POIs? It may not be relevant to the technical issue you’re asking about, given what you’ve said; but in general this is changing the fit model, and typically not what would be appropriate from a physics point of view, and lead to different results. Of course it depends what you’re looking to do, which I don’t know.
I hope this helps,
Kyle
[1] https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/commonstatsmethods/#likelihood-fits-and-scans

Hi Stephanie,
Some first thoughts:
One difference between the two fits, in the first case (the MultiDimFit run with --algo none which is the default), the MultiDim fit is not reporting the uncertainties directly, its just doing the minimization.
By using -v 3 you’ve been able to check the uncertainties on the parameters. Unless I’m mistaken, these will be the uncertainties reported from MINUIT’s minimization step, from its error matrix, and hence are reported as symmetric intervals.
When running the impacts, the asymmetric intervals (using MINOS rather than the symmetric ones from the error matrix) are computed and then the impact on the POI at each of the 1 sigma bounds is determined at these points.
In the original (–algo none, default MultiDimFit) fit, its possible the fit never goes anywhere near these +/-1 sigma points. So it could be that things appear reasonably well-behaved when performing a the minimzation and taking the error matrix from the second derivative at the minimum, but that there are issues elsewhere in the fit.
As a first step I would suggest maybe running a scan of the NLL over the parameter(s) that are giving you trouble. [1]
e.g.
combine -M MultiDimFit --algo grid --points 100 -d <your_datacard> --P p1BTag
plot1DScan.py higgsCombineTest.MultiDimFit.mH120.root --POI p1BTag
with other customization options as necessary.
That will produce a 1D scan of the NLL for that parameter. It may show a large asymmetry, or some other pathology (such as a kink in the NLL) which may serve as a starting point to investigate what is going on, or what next steps might be taken.
As an aside, it’s not clear to me why in the original fit you are redefining all of your nuisance parameters as POIs? It may not be relevant to the technical issue you’re asking about, given what you’ve said; but in general this is changing the fit model, and typically not what would be appropriate from a physics point of view, and lead to different results. Of course it depends what you’re looking to do, which I don’t know.
I hope this helps,
Kyle
