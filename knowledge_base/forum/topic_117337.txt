QUESTION (by milee @ 2025-03-05T15:49:31.884Z):
Dear experts,
Recently we look into the unblind impact plot and observed some pulls are missing in the impact plot. As suggesting in the post[1], these nuisance has larger post-fit uncertainty which leads invalid pull definition. Following the recommendation, we have looked the likelihood scan for these nuisances. It does show larger post-fit uncertainties observed compared to the pre-fit, while no suspicious shape of the scan. We noticed that nuisance with invalid pulls are the ones with large uncertainties (above 50%), and we have a small signal for our analysis (HIG-24-009). Do you have any suggestion on how to understand these nuisance or it is an genuine effect?
likelihood.pdf (132.7 KB)
Here’s an example command we use to obtain the likelihood scan of the problematic nuisance.
combine -M MultiDimFit -n CMS_HIG24009_FS_hc --algo grid --redefineSignalPOIs r \
-P CMS_HIG24009_FS_hc --setParameterRanges r=-1000.00,1000.00:CMS_HIG24009_FS_hc=-2,2 \
--cminDefaultMinimizerStrategy 0 --robustFit 1 --X-rtd MINIMIZER_MaxCalls=99999999 \
--cminDefaultMinimizerTolerance 0.1 -m 125 -d cards/datacards/input/hc_emu_all_13TeV.root \
--setParameters r=1,rgx{CMS_SF_*.*}=1,CMS_HIG24009_FS_hc=0 \
--floatOtherPOIs 1 --saveInactivePOI 1

The results can be reproduced from this workspace[2].
