REPLY 2 (by vischia @ 2022-07-12T12:51:33.000Z):
Dear Sebastian,
for more detailed answers more information would be needed, I guess, but in general if you have a large background and hunt for a small signal, you can think of the background normalization as something that will dramatically impact on your signal strength if it fluctuates down (because you leave lots of space for the very small signal) but will have no impact on the signal strength if it fluctuates up (because once it has “eaten up” the signal, the signal strength will not have any reason to vary, because it has been already saturated. In such a case, you may find changes if you allow the xsec for the signal to go negative in the fit)
I hope this helps.
Cheers,
Pietro

REPLY 5 (by adewit @ 2022-07-12T20:46:45.079Z):
Dear Sebastian,
I think it would be best if you can try running the impacts with an appropriate range for these Dijet_p1_201X parameters (you can use e.g. --setParameterRanges Dijet_p1_2016=6,7). The error message you’re seeing means the interval on this parameter could not be determined, which can often happen when the minimizer takes too large a step in this parameter, and adjusting the range helps the minimizer determine a more appropriate step size.
Best,
Adinda
