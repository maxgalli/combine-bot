REPLY 3 (by kcormier @ 2024-07-29T08:41:06.078Z):
Hi Sasha,
The --cminDefaultMinimizerTolerance sets how close the minimizer needs to think it is to the true minimum in order to consider that a fit has succeeded. The minimizer estimates an “estimated distance to minimum” (EDM) and requires this to be below a certain threshold in order to consider a fit succesful. Exactly what role this is playing in your fit is not clear to me without more information.
When you say “a significance much larger than 5.0”, is that, e.g. 7? Or is it e.g. 30k? In some cases, such as for negative pdf values the program may perform a fast exit and return a very large NLL. I’m not sure if this is whats happening your case. If the values are very large, I suspect its more likely that this is due to some mode of fit failure than any information about the “true” deltaNLL.
Best,
Kyle

REPLY 5 (by kcormier @ 2024-07-29T09:49:55.412Z):
Hi Sasha,
As a first step, I would suggest you look at the postfit predictions for those fits. That should give you a sense pretty quickly which of those fits is actually describing the data more accurately and which has issues and what the fit is actually finding.
Depending on the frequency of the large DeltaNLL and the unstable behaviour of the significance though, it could be that the changes when you change the tolerance are a symptom of a larger instability in the fit or fit model rather than the real issue itself.
