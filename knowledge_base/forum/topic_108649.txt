QUESTION (by shin @ 2025-02-04T06:31:29.463Z):
Dear Combine Experts,
My analysis runs shape method of Combine Tools.
I am working on signal injection test where I inject various signal strength corresponding to ±2σ,  ±1σ, and median limit in blinded limit calculation.
I run MultiDimFit with following options:
–toysFile toy.root
–toysFrequentist
-t 50
–expectSignal ??? (change accordingly)
–cminDefaultMinimizerStrategy 1
–robustFit 1
–rMin -15 --rMax 15
–algo singles
When I draw the pull distribution (rtruth-rfit)/σfit, I constantly get bias towards negative mean in -2σ, -1σ, and median signal strength injection, where the bias moves towards 0 as the injected signal strength increases.
For example I get mean of toy runs like, -0.79, -0.54, -0.2, -0.06, 0.01 for -2σ, -1σ, median, +1σ, +2σ respectively.
I have tried different options like narrowing down the r range to [-2,2], and changed --cminDefaultMinimizerStrategy 0 and --robustFit 0 to expedite the fitting process, as I have many nuisance parameters. Making both changes did not make much difference.
I want to ask for an advice on how to alleviate this bias.
If there truly is a bias, what should I do to remove the bias? Change the binning of the input?
Thank you.
Sincerely,
Jihoon Shin
