REPLY 4 (by adewit @ 2025-03-28T13:57:55.235Z):
Dear Byeonghak,
Not sure if your issue was resolved in the meantime, but if not, a few thoughts: in principle you expect the total normalization of your signal+background yields to be constrained to sqrt(number of data events). If the total pre-fit uncertainty is larger than sqrt(number of data events), the uncertainty will thus be constrained in the fit. This does not necessarily mean that nuisance parameters need to be constrained, nuisance parameters becoming anti-correlated in the fit will also lead to the overall normalization being constrained.
This means that if you removed nuisance parameters that were strongly anti-correlated with the luminosity uncertainty, the effect that you describe is not so surprising: by removing a lever for constraining the total rate uncertainty (ie the anticorrelations), in the fit the nuisance parameters would simply be constrained further.
To give an even more extreme example, if the luminosity uncertainty was the only uncertainty that was applied, it would very likely be constrained, such that the overall rate would be constrained to the appropriate level in the fit.
This does not answer the question of what should be done, of course. Is the overall scale of the signal a freely floating parameter (rateParam), or is it implemented as a log-normal constraint? If it’s not a freely floating parameter, I would expect that making it freely floating would reduce the constraint that you see.
Apart from this, it is worth seeing if there may be any nuisance parameters missing from your uncertainty model - we don’t normally expect to constrain the luminosity uncertainty, partly because there are usually other parameters that affect only the total rate of the processes, which would introduce a (partial) anticorrelation with this parameter. So, if the model has “too few” parameters, I think you could run into the effect that you are seeing.
Hope this helps a bit,
Adinda
