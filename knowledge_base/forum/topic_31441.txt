QUESTION (by fmonti @ 2023-11-08T14:42:51.726Z):
Dear combine experts,
We are performing a likelihood scan of a certain POI using the combine method MultiDimFit with the grid algorithm. In a non-negligible fraction of points of the grid we get the following messages at the end of the log:
....
INFO: (function: improveOnce) CascadeMinimizer.cc: 188 -- Minimisation configured with Type=Minuit2, Algo=Migrad, strategy=0, tolerance=0.1
INFO: (function: improveOnce) CascadeMinimizer.cc: 211 -- Minimisation finished with status=-1
DEBUG: (function: improveOnce) CascadeMinimizer.cc: 217 -- Minimization ended with latest status != 0 or 1

What is the meaning of status=-1? Also the last message suggests that acceptable statuses are 0 and 1, do I understand correctly? Thank you.
Best regards,
Fabio

REPLY 2 (by anigamov @ 2023-11-09T10:43:41.578Z):
Hi Fabio,
The status=-1 means that the fit has not converged for that particular point, status=1 means that the covariance matrix ended up not positive definite and status=0 means everything is fine.
Depending on the model this could happen and does not necessarily mean that you have a problem with the fit. You mention a non-negligible fraction of points, but are these points close to the POIs boundaries, or rather in the middle of the 2D scan?
First, I would suggest adding the -v 3 option, it will increase the verbosity level and should  help you to understand what is happening.
If you don’t see anything striking from the log you could add the --X-rtd MINIMIZER_MaxCalls=9999999 option which increases the number of maximum calls for the minimisation.
Best,
Aliya
