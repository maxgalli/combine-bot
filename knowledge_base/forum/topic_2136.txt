QUESTION (by Philip_Daniel_Keiche @ 2021-08-04T15:16:02.000Z):
*** Discussion title: Higgs Combination
Dear combine experts,
Recently, I read up on the likelihood unfolding in the documentation
[1]. While reading, I had the impression that the approach is very
similar to a STXS measurement - you define bins on gen level, generate
the corresponding statistical model for a given observable and perform a
fit of all gen bins simultaneously. The only differences I can think of
are
- in STXS, we measure cross sections, not the signal strength modifier
and
- in the LL unfolding, we add regularization to stabilize the unfolding
process.
So if I understand correctly, I should be able to convert datacards used
for STXS measurements to datacards for LL unfolding by adding the
regularization parameters. Is that correct? Is there a more
sophisticated difference, or a conceptional reason not to do this at
all? Sorry if this is a very obvious question, just want to make sure
that I understand this correctly.
Any feedback would be much appreciated, thank you!
Cheers, Philip
[1]
https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part3/regularisation.html

REPLY 2 (by adewit @ 2021-08-04T15:33:57.000Z):
*** Discussion title: Higgs Combination
Hi Philip,
That's correct, when you're doing an STXS measurement you are already
unfolding in the likelihood, it is just unregularized.
The difference of whether you're measuring a signal strength modifier or
a cross section is mainly down to whether you include theoretical
uncertainties that scale the overall cross section in the model (signal
strength) or not (cross section).
Note that there is no obligation to use regularization when you unfold
(though you may decide that it's necessary depending on the case).
Best,
Adinda
