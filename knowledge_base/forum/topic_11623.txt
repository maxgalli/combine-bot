QUESTION (by atarabin @ 2022-06-13T15:12:59.146Z):
Dear Combine experts,
We are working on the 1st step of the unblinding procedure of HIG-21-009 (fiducial XS in HZZ4l). The impacts for all observables look good (e.g. https://atarabin.web.cern.ch/atarabin/fiducial/1stStepUnbl/massZ1/impacts_skimmed_v3_massZ1_r_smH_massZ1_0_asimov.pdf), but for two observables we notice something strange in the pulls of some nuisances, particularly CMS_eff_e, which is our leading nuisance (https://atarabin.web.cern.ch/atarabin/fiducial/1stStepUnbl/rapidity4l/impacts_skimmed_v3_rapidity4l_r_smH_YH_0_asimov.pdf). They are very shifted from zero, e.g. CMS_eff_e = 3.5.
We had a look at the workspaces and at the general set-up but we have not noticed anything different from all the other observables. We also investigated the correlations of CMS_eff_e, but they are reasonable and cannot explain what we see.
Do you have any insight of the problem? Perhaps you can suggest us some tests to run.
You can find here [1] the card and ws to reproduce the error. The comands used to generate the impacts can be found here [2] and reflect the standard procedure.
Best,
Alessandro Tarabini
[1] https://gitlab.cern.ch/cms-hcg/cadi/hig-21-009/-/tree/master/rapidity4l
[2] https://atarabin.web.cern.ch/atarabin/fiducial/1stStepUnbl/rapidity4l/commands_impacts_rapidity4l_v3.py

REPLY 3 (by atarabin @ 2022-06-15T15:57:46.000Z):
Hello Andrea,
Thanks for your reply.
Yes, we have some recent presentations. This is the last update from last week [1], in the first slides you can find the link to all other presentations.
About the problem of the shifted pulls we have found the solution. There was a subtle and sneaky bug in the computation of the templates for the ZX background. This problem was causing the ZX NP to be shifted and because of the correlations among NPs, the impacts looked that way. We have fixed the problem and now everything is fine [2]

[2] https://atarabin.web.cern.ch/atarabin/fiducial/1stStepUnbl/rapidity4l/
Cheers,
Alessandro
