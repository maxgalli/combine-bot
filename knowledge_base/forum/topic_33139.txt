QUESTION (by acarvalh @ 2024-01-09T12:08:10.844Z):
Dear experts
In the context of B2G-23-005 (combination of X → HH/HY searches), we are finding a mass region where there is a double peak structure in the GOF.
In this analysis, we have performed statistical tests only for a couple of mX choices the X → HH case, as the X → HY case incorporates a subset of the analyses that make the X → HH case. The mX cases in which we tested the GOF were 280 GeV, 500 GeV, and 1 TeV. The only place where we found the double peak issue is in the 500 GeV case.
The slides attached show some tests we had made to understand the issue.
In special we find that:
1 - The effect is seen in the mass range mX = 450 GeV - 550 GeV, where the HH multilepton (HIG-21-002) and bbWW (HIG-21-005) analyses are competitive (slide 3). In the HH multilepton case alone there is a hint of a double-peak structure
2 - That is exactly an effect of the interplay between these two analyses (slide 4), but
3 - no outstanding effect is seen in the pulls (slide 5).
4 - The upper limit result with toys agrees with the asymptotic one.
It is not feasible to perform all the fits in the HibridNew method (considering the two scenarios we have more than 100 mass points)
We would like to consult with you if the present results are satisfactory, or if any additional test on the robustness of the Assimptotic limit result can be done
cheers
Xanda, Chu, and Rainer for the B2G-23-005 team

REPLY 2 (by anigamov @ 2024-01-09T12:48:11.652Z):
Dear Xanda,
First, I would suggest to produce a likelihood scan for the signal strength for the combined bbWW and multi-lepton datacard, it could be that there is a double minima in the likelihood and the uncertainties are not accurate.
Then to investigate further one could compare the impacts produced with bbWW and multi-lepton separately and with combined datacard, perhaps a difference in pulls would point to the origin of the observed tension.

REPLY 4 (by acarvalh @ 2024-01-12T20:54:29.786Z):
Hi Aliya
We performed further investigations and ended up finding a silly mistake in the fitting procedure: we were mistakenly injecting 100 times the expected signal strength into the fit
Once that mistake was corrected we found a one-bump GOF with ~ 30-60 % p-value to all tested mass points
We are sorry for bothering you, thank you for your support
