QUESTION (by amaria @ 2025-08-21T22:36:46.653Z):
Hi everyone,
I am currently working with CMS Combine and I am using it for applying discrete profiling to some model and then running  AsymptoticLimits  for setting an upper limit. We are also running a likelihood scan using MultiDimFit, and this is where things get weird. In some of the mass ranges we are studying, the envelope has a very weird shape and does not actually just follow the least-NLL PDF. I have attached an image for clarity. What I am plotting is 2*(deltaNLL+nll+nll0) as I found in the documentation.
nll_d86_m1.188_bias_correction1396×872 47.7 KB
The Combine command I am running is:
combine -M MultiDimFit -d my_datacard.txt -m X.XXX -n _d_suffix --algo grid --points 70 -v 1 –setParameters pdf_index=-1  –freezeParameters parameters,of,gaussian  –setParameterRanges r=-0.35,0.35 --cminDefaultMinimizerStrategy 0 --cminDefaultMinimizerTolerance=0.001 --robustFit=1 --saveNLL --X-rtd REMOVE_CONSTANT_ZERO_POINT=1  --saveWorkspace --redefineSignalPOIs r --X-rtd MINIMIZER_freezeDisassociatedParams
For the envelope, whereas for the individual PDFs, pdf_index is set to a certain value and then frozen. I have tried a bunch of options, like changing the strategy, the number of points, increasing tolerance or try some other suggested options in the documentation, but nothing works. Why am I getting this weird behavior? I made sure most of the points for all PDFs have a converging fit.
Thank you

REPLY 2 (by gortona @ 2025-08-22T07:04:13.000Z):
Dear Andrea,
This kind of behaviour sometimes happens when there is a second (local) minima in the likelihood shape, and for some unfortunate set of parameters the fit procedure ends up there.
Usually it can be cured by some tuning of the allowed ranges of the fit parameters. Running lilkelihood scans for individual parameters or looking at the log output of the failing MultidimFit points for some parameters with extreme values might help identify the culprits.
But in your case, may I ask if this option:

–freezeParameters parameters,of,gaussian

is actually intended as it is? You really have 3 parameters in your workspaces called parameters, of, and gaussian, respectively?
Best regards,
Giacomo
