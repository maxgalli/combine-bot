REPLY 2 (by byates @ 2024-01-19T13:40:18.000Z):
Hi Antonio,
May I ask what PAG you are performing this analysis under? If you are working within the TOP PAG, we’d love to have you present at a TMP meeting, or maybe ttX.
In general, we recommend using the Higgs Combine tool [1] to perform statistical analysis. Depending on what you are trying to measure, this can be a simple as creating a datacard containing event counts [2], or something more sophisticated like templates or a custom physics model to account for parameters like Wilson coefficients.
I’m sure you can iterate with the CAT experts here (I’m not one of them, so they may have more to say), and you can also reach out to the PAG conveners, again in whichever group you are performing the analysis under.
[1] https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/
[2] https://cms-analysis.github.io/HiggsAnalysis-CombinedLimit/part2/settinguptheanalysis/

Hi Antonio,
That is correct, “bins” in Combine datacard language refer to the analysis categories, these numbers are not strictly required and Combine can calculate them from the information you provide in the main body of your datacard. To setup the datacard for shape analysis you can follow instructions from Combine tutorial here and also consult the page with general instructions here.
