REPLY 4 (by gortona @ 2023-10-05T08:16:11.534Z):
Dear Amandeep,
If I read correctly the plot you sent, it seems that r is bounded to be positive in your model. Is that correct? If so, you might try to use  --setParameterRanges r=0.00001,20 to constrain properly the allowed phase space.
As a side note, the assumption the the likelihood ratio behaves like a chi2 distribution does not hold near the boundary. I’m not sure algo singles will give you the right 68%/95% interval there. A safer approach would be to use toys to estimate the lower intervals.
Best regards,
Giacomo

REPLY 6 (by agilbert @ 2023-10-06T08:20:45.891Z):
Hi Amandeep,
I think the answer is in the shape of the likelihood curve:

Amandeep Kaur:

plot_1200_1dscan698×475 12.5 KB
plot_1200_1dscan698×475 12.5 KB

The curve is flattening towards large values of r, and so at least within the range scanned (0-10) it is not crossing the threshold for the 95% CL (when 2*deltaNLL = 3.84).
The reason for this behaviour depends on the physics model, but can happen e.g. with parameterisations involving ratios of signal rates.
You could try extending rMax by a large amount (but it may not work if deltaNLL is asymptotically approaching a value < 3.84).
cheers,
Andrew
